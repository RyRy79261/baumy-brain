## Predictive-processing / generative reconstruction
> You don't have memories, you have a model that generates them on demand: recall is reconstruction under a prior, forgetting is the compression that makes the model generalize, and imagining the future is the same act pointed the other way.

**Score** — data: better · objective: better · novelty: genuinely-alternative
**Verdict:** The strongest reorganizing theorem yet fielded for the conversational objective, and its self-scoring is unusually honest. On data it is better, not merely comparable: it explains the entire reconstructive tradition (Bartlett distortion, DRM lures, Loftus misinformation, Schacter constructive simulation) as THE mechanism with direction-specific, quantitative predictions (Hemmer & Steyvers), and it makes a confirmed cross-domain prediction the store frame structurally cannot — that damaging the episodic generator impairs imagining the future and constructing novel scenes, not just retrieval (Hassabis 2007). One compression principle unifies forgetting curves, gist/verbatim, scripts, infantile amnesia, and oral-tradition mass recall that the incumbent stipulates separately. The incumbent's symbolic store + trust-gating + AES-encrypted secrets is genuinely superior here, and the frame must concede a verbatim/verification layer — it cannot be purely generative. The incumbent's soft-hide/purge with row ids is a real, not rhetorical, win.; Unfalsifiability / mechanistic under-specification risk. 'It is all one generative model' can explain anything post hoc; the frame is strong exactly where it makes the sharp predictions above and weak as a general solvent. ## Communicative / relevance-theoretic memory

**Verdict:** A genuine alternative organizing principle — not a mere reweighting of the incumbent's relevance term. ## Social-relational / transactive memory
> Memory is not a store you read from but a relationship you maintain: its unit is "us," its default operation is collaborative reconstruction for the present, and its crown jewel is knowing who or where knowledge lives — not holding the knowledge.

**Score** — data: better · objective: better · novelty: genuinely-alternative
**Verdict:** STRONGEST OBJECTIVE-FIT OF ANY COMPETITOR TO THIS TASK, but a complement to the incumbent's core, not a replacement for it. The objective literally names the failure mode this theorem is built to kill: "recalls naturally rather than as robotic database lookups." Assert-top-k over a weighted KG IS that failure mode; collaborative reconstruction, hedging/repair, present-orientation, and an honest who-knows-what directory are the direct antidotes, and they are structurally unreachable from a fidelity-store incumbent — so fitToObjective is better, decisively.

On fitToData it is better against THIS incumbent specifically: a symbolic-store + weighted-KG model is a fidelity model, yet the largest, best-replicated bands of human memory science are fidelity-FAILURES the store must bolt on as error modes — the misinformation effect, memory conformity (Gabbert's 71% co-witness adoption), collaborative recall/inhibition non-monotonicity, Bartlett schema leveling-sharpening, DRM/gist false memories, and the developmental co-construction of autobiography (Nelson & Fivush; infantile-amnesia offset tracking the onset of elaborative memory-talk). The social frame generates these as design, not patches. That is genuine explanatory gain.

But be skeptical of the "it all falls out" rhetoric — three real costs: (1) It is weaker, not stronger, on the cleanest neuropsych evidence: H.M.-style double dissociations and semantic-dementia-vs-amnesia point to genuinely separable substrates (striatal/hippocampal/neocortical), and recasting them as a single "social-distribution continuum" is asserted post-hoc — procedural memory survives amnesia because of its substrate, not because bike-riding is "non-transactive." (2) The comparative-cognition evidence actively undercuts the central claim: Clayton & Dickinson's solitary, non-linguistic scrub jays and Panoz-Brown's rats show structured what-where-when episodic memory with NO social/transactive machinery — the theorem can only absorb this by retreating to "reconstruction-ready structure," conceding the social unit is not necessary for the core episodic phenomenon. (3) The theorem's own honest admission that several phenomena (infantile amnesia, episodic-like structure) "complement rather than compete." So it trades bands rather than dominating globally; it wins the band that matters most for a conversational companion and for critiquing a fidelity-store incumbent.

The load-bearing caveat, which the built case states honestly and which caps how far the theorem can govern: its central operation — "track the shared account, defer to social input" — IS memory poisoning. In Baumy's threat model (privacy mode off, every group message attacker-controlled, "LLM proposes/code disposes") the reconstructive-conformable layer must be firewalled from the precision/security core (wifi/door/bank/reminder times stay in the exact encrypted deterministic store) or it weakens the injection wall. It is also mechanistically thin — it hands you no retrieval equation the way RRF or ACT-R activation does — and pays off only after shared history accumulates (weak cold-start, weak single-dyad). Correct verdict: adopt it as the GOVERNING theory of the relational/conversational layer sitting ATOP the incumbent's exact store, not instead of it. Net: genuinely alternative organizing principle, best-in-class for the objective, but explicitly two-layer — its own boundary condition is what saves it from being dangerous.
**Strongest contribution:** The transactive who-knows-what directory promoted to a first-class, volunteered output: "I don't hold that — Marta does / it's written on the fridge / you told me Tuesday." It is the one idea that is simultaneously (a) genuinely novel versus the incumbent (which has honest-miss knows/partial/blank for its OWN store, but no cross-agent metamemory directory over housemates + external stores + the AI itself), (b) uniquely apt for Baumy's actual setting — a GROUP memory across a house, exactly where a distributed metamemory beats a private item-store, (c) directly objective-serving: it converts "I don't know" into information, cuts confabulation, and reads as a trustworthy housemate rather than a failed lookup, and (d) safe — it operates on pointers and provenance, not on reconstructing security-critical content, so it delivers the theorem's benefit without crossing the poisoning boundary that makes the rest of the social frame dangerous. Metamemory-over-content is the theorem's highest-leverage, lowest-risk export.

**Beats incumbent:** Naturalness of recall — co-reconstruction, hedging, and present-orientation are exactly the objective; the incumbent's assert-top-k reads as a database lookup, which is the failure mode the objective names.; Remembers 'us,' not just facts — a companion's core need is relationship + shared history as the stored unit, which a single-item weighted store structurally under-models.; Fits Baumy's actual setting — this is a GROUP memory across housemates, so a transactive who-knows-what directory is a more faithful model than a personal-item store, and it yields less confabulation and more trust via honest metamemory.; Meets user expectations about their own memory — humans recall conversations as gist, reconstructively, and socially-updated; the frame works with that grain instead of fighting it.; Explains as core what the incumbent bolts on as exceptions — misinformation, memory conformity, collaborative recall, the emergence of autobiography, oral-tradition prodigies, and childhood amnesia all fall out of the theory rather than needing patches.
**Loses:** Precision-critical facts: reconstructive, gist-based, socially-conformable memory is actively wrong for wifi passwords, door codes, bank details, and reminder times. The incumbent's exact encrypted triple store with exact recall is correct there; Baumy's crypto + exact retrieval must remain the authority for these.; Security: memory conformity IS memory poisoning. 'Track the shared account, defer to social input' is precisely the attack surface Baumy's trust tiers, injection wall, and 'LLM proposes, code disposes' golden rule exist to defend. Taken naively the social frame makes memory MORE responsive to untrusted group text — it weakens the wall rather than reinforcing it.; Mechanistic underspecification: transactive/collective-memory theory is strong on function and phenomenology but weak on algorithm — it hands you no retrieval equation the way ACT-R activation or RRF does. Harder to implement and harder to make deterministic.; Testability/reproducibility: reconstruction-that-varies resists unit-testing; Baumy's offline deterministic PGlite suite and exactly-once reminder path depend on determinism the social frame does not supply.; Cold start / single dyad: with no shared history and no partner directory, the frame has little to say. ## Rate-distortion / predictive-compression
> Memory is a capacity-limited predictive codec: it stores the minimal bits needed to reconstruct what a task will make you need, so remembering is generative decoding and forgetting is optimal lossy compression — the same objective a language model already runs.

**Score** — data: better · objective: better · novelty: genuinely-alternative
**Verdict:** A powerful, empirically-grounded competing theory that wins BOTH axes but cannot stand alone for this deployment. On the canonical memory evidence it is clearly better than the symbolic-store incumbent: it unifies working-memory precision, the forgetting curve, consolidation, and — its load-bearing coup — derives reconstructive distortion / false memory (Bartlett, DRM, fuzzy-trace) as OPTIMAL decoding toward the prior with a quantitative law (error ~ low-rate x strong-prior; Nagy/Torok/Orban 2020), where the store model calls all of these failures and bolts on separate error terms. On the naturalness objective it is the only frame that explains WHY reconstructive recall sounds human and lookup robotic, and it prescribes the concrete fix (dual-code: gist reconstruction constrained by a verbatim anchor). The asymmetry that decides novelty and objective-fit: RD can absorb exact facts as a high-beta / infinite-distortion operating point (keep verbatim), while the store has no theory of the naturalness half at all — no account of reconstruction, graceful gist decay, shared dyadic codebooks, or inference of never-stated facts. So the correct verdict is not 'replace the KG' but 'the KG is RD's licensed high-fidelity life-raft': adopt rate-distortion as the ORGANIZING theorem (bit-allocation by relational distortion cost, per-person/dyad generative model as the central object, surprise-gated retention, forgetting-as-re-compression), and keep the symbolic append-supersede store as its exact-recall and audit subsystem. It is the strongest of the competing memory theorems for a conversational companion — genuinely alternative, not subsumable — provided it is deployed as a hybrid rather than a monolith.
**Strongest contribution:** It recasts reconstructive, gist-based, sometimes-wrong recall — the very thing that makes recall sound human rather than robotic — as the OPTIMAL signature of a capacity-limited lossy predictive codec, not a bug: retrieval is generative decoding from a per-person/dyad prior, so confabulation-toward-prior, gist-over-verbatim, and schema intrusion are the design target (with a quantitative distortion law), and the human/LLM correspondence becomes a mathematical identity — the same predictive-compression objective that produces fluent conversation IS the memory model — which makes the per-person generative model and shared codebook the central data structures and prescribes the dual-code fix (gist reconstruction + verbatim life-raft) that directly serves conversational naturalness.

**Beats incumbent:** Explains systematic distortion, false memory, and schema intrusion AS OPTIMAL, and quantitatively (Nagy, Török & Orbán 2020; Bartlett; DRM; fuzzy-trace) — the store model calls these failures and bolts on separate error terms. For a companion, this reframes 'natural, gist-based recall' from a liability into the design goal.; Unifies working-memory precision, the forgetting curve, consolidation, and semantic/episodic interplay under ONE objective with few parameters (R, β, p) — versus the incumbent's independent mechanisms (slots, decay constants, an activation equation, CLS interleaving) each fit separately. Fewer moving parts, one knob set.; Makes the human/LLM correspondence literal and load-bearing: both are predictive compressors, so the theory and the implementation substrate share the same math. ## Dynamical-systems / attractor-trajectory
> Memory is not a store of items but the sculpted flow of a continuous state space — to remember is to settle into an attractor basin, so recall is reconstruction-by-dynamics, and the incumbent's items-plus-graph is just the far-apart, frozen-landscape limit of this: right for the safety layer, wrong for the conversational one.

**Score** — data: better · objective: better · novelty: genuinely-alternative
**Verdict:** The strongest challenger to date. It is the mainstream computational-neuroscience account of biological associative memory, and it genuinely out-explains the incumbent on the reconstructive core of human recall: pattern completion and cue-dependence are native rather than bolted-on context links; reconstructive distortion is predicted in DIRECTION (which DRM lure, which schema) from landscape topology, not just in presence; and reconsolidation plus representational drift outright FALSIFY a fixed-address record store while being native corollaries here. It is a genuinely alternative ontology (memory=basin, recall=settling), not a relabeling, and its subsumption claim — items+graph as the far-apart/frozen/discrete limit — is defensible even if 'exact theorem' overstates the rigor. **Loses:** Auditability, exact deletion, and provenance — the objective's security core. The injection wall, trust tiers, confirm-tap 'forget', encrypted exact secrets, audit log, and exactly-once reminders all REQUIRE discrete, addressable, individually-deletable, source-tagged records with hard guarantees. Attractor superposition is the enemy of this: you cannot cleanly delete one memory from an overlapping landscape (unlearning leaves residue), cannot point to 'the row that grounded this claim', and cannot guarantee a secret is not reconstructable by pattern completion. The incumbent's precise store + trust-gated supersession + exact retrieval is simply correct for these paths, and the honest synthesis is to reconstruct the relationship but keep a deterministic exact store for the facts.; Auditability, testability, exactly-once: discrete facts/triples with trust tiers, supersession, and audit trails are inspectable, unit-testable, and governable. A reconstruct-at-read engine is hard to answer 'why did it say that?' for, hard to make deterministic in tests, and hard to make exactly-once — real liabilities for a security-sensitive bot.; Memory poisoning: integrating ALL available constraints IS the misinformation effect by construction, so this frame is more vulnerable to adversarial poisoning and undermines the incumbent's trust-gated defense (a fact supersedes only if its trust ≥ the incumbent's). Under an untrusted-group-input threat model this is a serious strike against reconstruction-primacy.; Consolidation mechanism specificity: Complementary Learning Systems (McClelland, McNaughton & O'Reilly, 1995) is a concrete, well-supported computational account (dual fast/slow learners solving catastrophic interference). 'Schematization' is more description than mechanism and in practice borrows CLS's machinery — here the incumbent has the better-specified model.; Principled deletion / right-to-be-forgotten / secret redaction: exact, reversible, auditable removal of specific rows is straightforward for a discrete store and genuinely hard when items have dissolved into gist and weights — you cannot cleanly delete a constraint already baked into a profile. The incumbent's row-scoped soft/purge delete is cleaner and safer.; For ordinary bookkeeping (reminders, rosters, config, grants) the discrete legible knowledge graph is simply the right tool; dressing it up as reconstruction adds risk and cost with no benefit. The frame earns its keep on the relational/companion surface, not the ledger.
