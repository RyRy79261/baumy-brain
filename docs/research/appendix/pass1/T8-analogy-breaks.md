# T8-analogy-breaks — Where the LLM <-> human analogy breaks down

**Topic verdict:** mixed

## Adjudicated claims

### A: LLM memory is bifurcated into two substrates (parametric weights vs non-parametric KV-cache/context) that degrade by categorically different, write-driven (not time-driven) laws — interference/spectral-collapse vs eviction/positional-starvation — and humans have no clean parametric/non-parametric split.
- **Verdict:** weakened-but-stands (confidence: high)
- **Strongest support:** The hard architectural gate is real and un-attacked by either side: at inference weights are frozen (cannot be written by experience) and context is volatile (cannot persist past the window). Biological memory instantiates neither property — under CLS (McClelland, McNaughton & O'Reilly 1995, Psych Review) the fast/slow systems are a continuous consolidation gradient, not a frozen store plus a discarded buffer. The 'writes not time' reframing (interference is write-count-driven; eviction is occupancy-driven; neither substrate has a native decay-over-time term) is a durable, genuinely disanalogous observation, since human forgetting does carry a real time/consolidation axis.
- **Strongest attack:** The vivid degradation-law framing is individually attackable on all three named mechanisms. (1) Spectral/rank collapse (Dong, Cordonnier & Loukas 2021, ICML, 2103.03404) is a theorem for pure attention WITHOUT residuals or MLPs — the paper's actual thesis ('Attention is NOT All You Need') is that residual paths + MLPs prevent the collapse, which every deployed transformer has; presenting it as a deployed-substrate degradation law inverts the paper. (2) 'Abrupt and complete' catastrophic interference is contested at LLM scale — Ramasesh, Lewkowycz & Dyer (ICLR 2022) find larger pretrained transformers are MORE forgetting-resistant, though Luo et al. (2308.08747) find the opposite for 1–7B continual fine-tuning, so the field is split. (3) 'Lost in the Middle' (Liu et al., TACL 2024) is substantially a calibratable positional-attention bias (Yu et al., 'Found in the Middle,' ACL 2024 Findings, 2406.16008) and StreamingLLM's perplexity explosion is abolished by retaining a few sink tokens — a fixable engineering choice, not an intrinsic substrate law.
- **Open question:** After subtracting the calibratable positional bias and the sink-token fix, is there any irreducible occupancy/eviction term left in the non-parametric substrate — i.e. does a genuine substrate-specific 'failure law' survive at all, or does only the frozen-weights/volatile-buffer architectural gate remain as the disanalogy?

### B: 'Where is a fact stored?' may be ill-posed in LLMs — edit success is uncorrelated with causal-tracing localization (Hase et al. 2023), and the knowledge-neuron thesis (Dai et al. 2022) is empirically undercut.
- **Verdict:** genuinely-contested (confidence: medium)
- **Strongest support:** The narrow, measured, reproducible result is solid: Hase, Bansal, Kim & Ghandeharioun (NeurIPS 2023, 2301.04213) show causal-tracing read-locus is statistically uncorrelated with the best MLP layer to edit; Niu, Liu, Zhu & Penn (ICLR 2024 Spotlight) show edit methods perturb syntax, not just facts; and degenerate encodings (Chen et al., 2402.13731, AAAI-2024 venue unconfirmed) plus non-propagating edits (Cohen et al., RippleEdits, TACL 2024) jointly make the strong-form 'one fact = one neuron address' thesis untenable.
- **Strongest attack:** The red team's reversal is forceful: distributed, non-localized storage with read/lesion (≈read/edit) dissociation is the DEFAULT expectation from neuroscience (Lashley's engram search; modern distributed-ensemble/engram work). If both substrates are distributed and both dissociate read-locus from write/lesion-locus, then B is a point of CONVERGENCE, not a break — the steelman has it backwards relative to its own thesis. Hase establishes read-locus ≠ edit-locus (an engineering fact about cheapest intervention point in any redundant code), NOT that 'storage is ill-posed' or 'doesn't carve a joint' — that is a metaphysical escalation the result does not reach. Ripple-effect non-propagation indicts local edit OPERATORS, and humans also famously fail to propagate a correction to its inferential closure.
- **Open question:** Is the read/edit-locus dissociation genuinely shared with biological engrams (making B a convergence), or is the fact that it is quantified, reproducible, and operationally exploitable in LLMs an LLM-specific EPISTEMIC affordance that constitutes a real methodological disanalogy even if the underlying distributed structure is shared?

### C: A writable memory (RAG store / agent long-term memory) is an attack surface with no biological analogue — untrusted inference-time input causes durable, privileged, retrieved-as-trusted state, and there is no standard write-trust defense.
- **Verdict:** weakened-but-stands (confidence: high)
- **Strongest support:** The specific conjunction has no biological instance: externally writable at inference time + retrieved-and-acted-on with the same authority as internal knowledge + no salience/consolidation gate + attacker needs no privilege. PoisonedRAG (Zou et al., 2402.07867, USENIX Security 2025) drives attacker-chosen answers at >90% by injecting ~5 passages; MINJA (Dong et al., 2503.03704, 2025) reports a no-privilege user poisoning persistent memory through ordinary queries with records surviving session resets and model updates. Biological encoding is gated by attention, salience, neuromodulation, and time-gated consolidation — there is no open write port. The research-state point is checkable and correct: no settled write-trust/provenance primitive exists, only a 2025–2026 detector arms race.
- **Strongest attack:** 'No analogue' is absolutism doing rhetorical work. Shaw & Porter (2015, Psychological Science) implanted rich false memories of committing a crime in ~70% of subjects via suggestive interviewing — durable, retrieved-as-genuine, acted on with autobiographical authority: exactly the threat-model SHAPE (untrusted external input → durable, trusted-on-recall memory → downstream behavior) has a real cognitive instance. So the honest disanalogy is one of reliability, directness, and gating (probabilistic, subject-mediated reconstruction over repeated high-effort sessions vs a single verbatim insert()), not existence.
- **Open question:** Given that Shaw-style implantation shares the threat-model shape, is the LLM disanalogy best stated as a quantitative gap (reliability/directness/ungatedness) rather than a categorical one — and does any biological process exist that installs a verbatim, immediately-retrievable, full-trust record in a single exposure (which would collapse the break further)?

### D: Elegant 'LLM = classical memory model' equivalences (attention=modern Hopfield; SAE latents = the model's concepts) may be mathematical re-description, not demonstrated mechanism; and long-context-as-solvent-of-retrieval-structure is genuinely open.
- **Verdict:** weakened-but-stands (confidence: high)
- **Strongest support:** The meta-thesis is correct and important and survives both sides' scrutiny: an elegant equivalence raises a mechanism HYPOTHESIS but does not discharge the burden of showing the mechanism is USED. It is well-supported by SAE non-canonicity (feature splitting/absorption, seed-dependence; 2502.04878, 2502.16681, 2505.20254 — arXiv-only) showing the decomposition is a chosen basis, and the long-context-open sub-claim is correctly left humble, with 'Found in the Middle' actually strengthening the openness by showing the U-shape is partly calibratable so neither 'solvent' nor 'irreducible retrieval structure' is established.
- **Strongest attack:** Two exhibits are overstated. (1) Heap et al. (2501.17727) is materially misused: the paper's claim is that AGGREGATE auto-interpretability METRICS fail to distinguish trained from random transformers — the authors explicitly do NOT claim SAE features are decoder-side artifacts, so citing it as 'the single most damaging re-description result' repeats the exact 'vocabulary outruns evidence' error the claim warns against. (2) 'An identity is a re-description, therefore silent on the realized algorithm' proves too much — it would dissolve most computational-equivalence results; the Hopfield identity (Ramsauer et al., ICLR 2021) DOES constrain the realized computation (fixed points, capacity, retrieval dynamics are those of associative memory). The genuinely defensible false-equivalence is specifically the BIOLOGICAL leap (different substrate, learning rule, no key/value projection), not the math.
- **Open question:** Does the metric-failure Heap et al. actually demonstrate generalize to circuit-level / causal interpretability methods that do not rely on auto-interp scoring, and does the attention↔Hopfield function-class constraint get EXERCISED as the operative retrieval dynamic in trained models or merely bounds a latent capacity?

**Strawman attacks flagged:** C, point 2 ('persistence surviving resets/updates is an artifact of engineered RAG plumbing, not an LLM substrate property; the attack surface is an added affordance') is not an attack on 'no biological analogue' — the steelman already explicitly scoped C to the engineered inference-time write channel and deliberately excluded parametric/training-data poisoning. Observing that the surface is an engineered affordance arguably SUPPORTS 'no biological analogue' (biology instantiates no such affordance) rather than undercutting it; the red team even concedes this ('That is fine for the thesis'). It is a scoping clarification masquerading as a rebuttal.; The red team's own framing of A's surviving core ('frozen-weights + volatile-buffer gate') slightly under-credits the steelman, which explicitly identified that same gate as 'the defensible core' — so 'downgrade from two failure laws to one gate' partly attacks a stronger degradation-law reading than the steelman's own hedged 'deep on parametric, shallow-to-absent on non-parametric' verdict already conceded.

**Overstated enrichment flagged:** Presenting spectral/rank collapse (Dong, Cordonnier & Loukas 2021) as 'a real, provable mechanism' of the PARAMETRIC substrate of a deployed transformer — the theorem holds only for pure attention with no residual connections and no MLPs, and the paper's thesis is that those components PREVENT the collapse. This is the steelman's crispest 'provable mechanism' claim and it inverts the source.; Transferring McCloskey & Cohen's (1989) 'abrupt and complete' catastrophic loss to LLM scale as if it carried cleanly, when the modern picture is contested-to-reversed (Ramasesh et al. ICLR 2022 find scale increases resistance).; Framing 'Lost in the Middle' as an occupancy/position 'substrate degradation law,' when a training-free calibration (Yu et al., ACL 2024 Findings) recovers much of the loss — indicating a substantial implementation/measurement-artifact component.; B's escalation from Hase's measured read-locus ≠ edit-locus to 'the storage locus may not carve the system at a joint' / a genuine DISANALOGY — the result licenses only the falsification of strong localism, and the distributed-engram parallel makes the broader claim plausibly a convergence.; Using Heap et al. # STEELMAN: Where the LLM ↔ human memory analogy genuinely breaks down

Scope note: research-landscape analysis only. Every paper below was checked this session for title/authors/venue via web search or arXiv unless explicitly flagged UNVERIFIED. Where a search summary asserted a venue/year I couldn't independently confirm, I flag it.

## Claim A — Two substrates, two failure modes; humans lack the clean split

**Strongest formulation.** In a deployed transformer, "memory" is physically bifurcated into two substrates with categorically different write-semantics and categorically different degradation laws, and that bifurcation is *architectural*, not metaphorical:

- **Parametric substrate (weights).** Written only by gradient descent. Its failure mode under sequential writes is *interference / representational overwrite*, and — in pure attention stacks — *rank collapse*. This is the well-established disanalogy to human learning at its origin: McCloskey & Cohen (1989, *Psychology of Learning and Motivation*) coined "catastrophic interference," showing connectionist nets abruptly and completely lose task A when trained on task B because the *same distributed weights* that encode A are repurposed for B. French (1999, *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*, "Catastrophic forgetting in connectionist networks") formalized it as the stability–plasticity dilemma intrinsic to distributed overlapping representations. Kirkpatrick et al. (2017, *PNAS*, "Overcoming catastrophic forgetting," EWC) confirmed it persists in deep nets and is governed by *which* weights move, i.e. by writes, not elapsed time. The spectral version: Dong, Cordonnier & Loukas (2021, *ICML*, "Attention is Not All You Need: Pure Attention Loses Rank Doubly Exponentially with Depth," arXiv:2103.03404) proved self-attention without skip connections/MLPs converges *doubly-exponentially* to a rank-1 matrix — a genuine "spectral collapse" toward token uniformity. This is a real, provable mechanism, not a loose analogy.

- **Non-parametric substrate (KV-cache / context).** Written by mere presence of tokens; ephemeral; discarded at session end. Its failure mode is *eviction and positional starvation*, again a function of writes/occupancy, not decay-over-time. Xiao et al. ("Efficient Streaming Language Models with Attention Sinks," arXiv:2309.17453, ICLR 2024 — *year flag: a search summary said "ICLR 2023," which is chronologically impossible for a Sept-2023 preprint; ICLR 2024 is correct*) show that once initial tokens are *evicted* from a fixed window, perplexity explodes catastrophically (Llama-2-13B ~5.4 → ~5158), and that a few "attention-sink" tokens must be retained to prevent collapse — a pure eviction pathology with no time term. Liu et al. ("Lost in the Middle," arXiv:2307.03172, *TACL* 2024) show retrieval accuracy is U-shaped in position: information in the middle of a long context is functionally starved even though it is *present and uncorrupted*. That is degradation as a function of *where/how much was written into the buffer*, orthogonal to any forgetting curve.

**Why the human side genuinely lacks this clean split.** The honest steelman is not "humans have no memory systems taxonomy" — they plainly do (declarative/procedural; episodic/semantic; hippocampal/neocortical). The disanalogy is sharper and survives scrutiny: the LLM divide is a **hard architectural gate** — weights are *frozen* at inference and *cannot* be written by experience; context is *volatile* and *cannot* persist past the window. Biological memory has neither property. Under Complementary Learning Systems theory (McClelland, McNaughton & O'Reilly, 1995, *Psychological Review*), the hippocampal "fast" and neocortical "slow" systems are **continuously coupled by systems consolidation** — the hippocampus repeatedly reinstates memories so neocortical synapses change "a little on each reinstatement." There is no frozen store and no discarded buffer; the two systems *bleed into each other by design*, on a graded timescale, during sleep and rest. So the human "two systems" are a consolidation *gradient*; the LLM "two substrates" are a *categorical inference-time wall*. The failure laws differ accordingly: human forgetting has a genuine time/consolidation axis (Ebbinghaus curves, retrograde-gradient amnesia); LLM parametric loss is write-count-driven interference and LLM context loss is occupancy/position-driven eviction — **neither has a native decay-over-time term at all.** That asymmetry is the defensible core of the claim.

**Convergence depth verdict: deep on the parametric side, shallow-to-absent on the non-parametric side.** Catastrophic interference is a *shared mechanism* between connectionist models and the CLS account of why brains evolved a hippocampus — genuine convergence, and CLS was literally motivated by connectionist forgetting. But the KV-cache/context substrate has *no* biological homolog: nothing in the brain is a frozen-parameter model reading from a discarded token buffer. Calling context "working memory" is where the analogy is weakest.

## Claim B — "Where is a fact stored?" may be ill-posed in LLMs

**Strongest formulation.** The strong "knowledge-neuron" reading — that a discrete fact lives at an identifiable, localized site whose modification *is* editing the fact — is empirically undercut from three independent directions, so the storage-locus question as usually posed is likely ill-formed.

1. **Localization ⟂ editability (the load-bearing result).** Hase, Bansal, Kim & Ghandeharioun, "Does Localization Inform Editing? Surprising Differences in Causality-Based Localization vs. Knowledge Editing" (arXiv:2301.04213, **NeurIPS 2023** — venue confirmed). Causal tracing (representation denoising, the method behind ROME) identifies *where a fact is carried in the forward pass*, yet the layer it fingers is **not** the best layer to edit; you get better edit success by *ignoring* the tracing result and always editing an early-to-mid MLP. Even for their "Fact Forcing" variant the correlation is weak. This is the crisp evidence that "where the model reads a fact" and "where you write to change it" are different questions — a dissociation with no clean analogue in a system where storage and update are the same substrate.

2. **The Knowledge-Neuron Thesis itself is contested.** Dai et al., "Knowledge Neurons in Pretrained Transformers" (ACL 2022, arXiv:2104.08696) is the seminal localization claim (MLPs as key-value fact stores). Niu, Liu, Zhu & Penn, "What does the Knowledge Neuron Thesis Have to do with Knowledge?" (**ICLR 2024, Spotlight** — confirmed) argue the KN thesis is an over-reading: the same edit methods perturb *linguistic/syntactic* phenomena, not just facts; MLP weights hold interpretable *patterns*, not stored *knowledge*, and any real account needs attention + layer structure, not a neuron address. Complementary evidence that localization is non-unique: Chen et al., "Cracking Factual Knowledge… Degenerate Knowledge Neurons" (arXiv:2402.13731; AAAI 2024 per search — *venue flag: not independently confirmed this session*) find *multiple distinct neuron sets* encode the *same* fact — degeneracy that breaks any "the fact is at address X" reading.
   - *UNVERIFIED, do not rely on:* one search summary asserted a "knowledge neurons route, not store" result (top-20 attributed neurons overlap a ~27-neuron routing circuit; knockout *raises* target probability ~8pp). I could not tie this to a specific verified paper/author/venue — treat as an unconfirmed lead, not evidence.

3. **Edits don't behave like stored facts.** Cohen, Biran, Yoran, Globerson & Geva, "Evaluating the Ripple Effects of Knowledge Editing" (arXiv:2307.12976, *TACL* 2024 — confirmed; RippleEdits, ~5K edits) show a "successful" edit fails to propagate to logically entailed facts (compositionality, aliasing, two-hop). A genuinely *stored* fact would carry its inferential closure; an LLM edit patches a surface locus without the graph moving with it. That is strong evidence the "fact = retrievable stored object at a place" frame imports a human/database intuition the substrate doesn't honor.

**Convergence depth verdict: this is a genuine disanalogy, honestly stated.** In human memory, "where is it stored" is *also* famously distributed (Lashley's engram search), so one must not overclaim novelty. The specifically-LLM force is the *operational dissociation* Hase demonstrates — read-locus ≠ write-locus, measured, reproducible — plus non-unique degenerate encodings and non-propagating edits. The defensible thesis is narrow and strong: **for LLMs, "the fact is stored at location L" is not just hard to answer but may not carve the system at a joint** — a stance directly supported by Hase (2023) and Niu et al. (2024).

## Claim C — A writable memory is an attack surface with no biological analogue

**Strongest formulation.** The moment memory is *externally writable at inference time* (RAG store, agent long-term memory), it becomes a security surface where untrusted input directly causes durable, privileged state — and there is no standard, principled write-trust defense, nor any real biological counterpart to "an attacker appends a record to your memory."

- **Seminal, verified:** Zou, Geng, Wang & Jia, "PoisonedRAG: Knowledge Corruption Attacks to RAG" (arXiv:2402.07867, Feb 2024; **USENIX Security 2025** per the authors' repo — *note: one search summary said "2024"; the project page/DBLP indicate USENIX Security 2025, arXiv 2024*). Injecting ~5 crafted passages per target question drives attacker-chosen answers at >90% success across LLMs/datasets, by *separately* optimizing a retrieval component (cosine-match the query) and a generation component (assert the false answer). The write channel *is* the exploit.
- **Agent long-term memory (sharper form):** Dong et al., "A Practical Memory Injection Attack against LLM Agents" (MINJA, arXiv:2503.03704, 2025; also on OpenReview — verified). A plain user with *no elevated privileges and no direct write access* poisons an agent's persistent memory *through ordinary queries*, >95% injection success via bridging steps + progressive prompt-shortening; the malicious record **survives session resets, context wipes, and model updates**. This is precisely the property that has no biological homolog.

**Why "no biological analogue" is defensible and not just rhetoric.** Human memory is emphatically *not* an open write port. Encoding requires attention, salience, emotional/neuromodulatory gating, and time-gated consolidation; there is no interface by which a third party *appends a durable, later-retrieved-as-trusted memory* to you by presenting text. The nearest phenomena — misinformation effect (Loftus), false-memory implantation, suggestion — are (a) probabilistic, (b) mediated by the subject's *own* reconstructive encoding, and (c) *not* privileged writes to a store the subject will later treat as ground truth on par with perception. Critically, brains have no equivalent of "an untrusted document is retrieved and then *acted on with the same authority as internal knowledge*." The disanalogy is structural: biological consolidation is a *trust-and-salience gate by construction*; a vector store's `insert()` is not. And the honest research-state observation that hardens the claim: **there is no consensus, standard write-trust/provenance defense** — the literature is a fast-moving arms race of detectors (activation-analysis and anomaly-detection proposals appear in 2025-2026 preprints), not a settled primitive.

**Convergence depth verdict: this is a true break, not a shared mechanism.** Where A and B feature *some* convergence, C is where the analogy is most clearly *absent*: externally-writable, retrieval-privileged memory is an engineered affordance with a security semantics (integrity, provenance, trust tiers) that biological memory simply does not instantiate. The steelman does not need to overreach — PoisonedRAG and MINJA are sufficient existence proofs of a threat model with no cognitive counterpart.

## Claim D — Elegant equivalences may be re-description, not mechanism

**Strongest formulation.** Several celebrated "LLM = classical memory model" equivalences are *mathematical re-descriptions of the same computation under a new name*, and do not, by themselves, license mechanistic or biological-memory claims. Two exhibits, plus one open problem.

- **attention ≈ modern Hopfield.** Ramsauer et al., "Hopfield Networks is All You Need" (arXiv:2008.02217, **ICLR 2021** — confirmed) prove the continuous modern-Hopfield update rule *is* transformer key-value attention. This is real and beautiful — but it is an *identity*, and identities are re-descriptions: showing attention can be *written as* associative-memory retrieval does not establish that the network *uses* associative-memory dynamics as its operative mechanism, nor that this maps onto biological associative recall. The honest reading: the equivalence constrains the *function class*; it is silent on the *realized algorithm*. (Compare: writing a sorting network as a matrix product doesn't make it "a linear-algebra memory.")
- **SAE features as "the model's concepts."** The interpretability program (Anthropic's "Towards Monosemanticity," Bricken et al. 2023; "Scaling Monosemanticity," Templeton et al. 2024 — *I did not re-verify these two this session; cite with that caveat*) reads sparse-autoencoder latents as the model's underlying features. Multiple 2025 results argue much of this is decoder-side re-description, not discovered mechanism:
  - Heap, Lawson, Farnik & Aitchison, "Sparse Autoencoders Can Interpret Randomly Initialized Transformers" (arXiv:2501.17727, Jan 2025 — verified). SAEs yield *similarly interpretable* latents and *similar quality metrics* on **untrained, random-Gaussian** transformers. If interpretable features fall out of architecture+data statistics even with no learned computation, then "the SAE found the model's concept" is not licensed — the interpretability may be partly artifact of the *method*, not the *model*. This is the single most damaging re-description result.
  - "Sparse Autoencoders Do Not Find Canonical Units of Analysis" (arXiv:2502.04878, 2025) and "Are Sparse Autoencoders Useful? A Case Study in Sparse Probing" (arXiv:2502.16681, 2025): feature splitting/absorption, initialization-dependence (different seeds → substantially different feature sets), and failure to beat strong baselines all indicate SAE decompositions are *non-unique* — i.e. a chosen basis, not a mechanism. (Also: "Position: Mechanistic Interpretability Should Prioritize Feature Consistency in SAEs," arXiv:2505.20254, 2025.) *These are arXiv preprints — flag as not-yet-archival-venue-verified.*
- **Genuinely open (do not resolve — steelman the openness):** whether long context *dissolves* the retrieval/memory structure — i.e. whether a sufficiently long in-context window makes explicit retrieval architecture unnecessary because the model attends over everything — is an **honestly unsettled** empirical question. "Lost in the Middle" (Liu et al., TACL 2024) is direct evidence that long context is *not* a clean uniform memory (U-shaped positional access), which argues *against* the "solvent" view; but the boundary conditions (with retrieval heads, with better positional schemes, at frontier context lengths) are not settled. The rigorous position is *epistemic humility*: this is open, and neither "long context replaces retrieval" nor "retrieval structure is irreducible" is currently established.

**Convergence depth verdict: this is the "beware shallow convergence" claim, and it is well-supported.** The attention/Hopfield identity is deep *mathematically* and shallow *mechanistically/biologically*; the SAE-feature story is where vocabulary ("features," "concepts," "memory") most outruns demonstrated mechanism, with Heap et al. (2025) the sharpest check. The defensible thesis: an elegant equivalence raises a *hypothesis* about shared mechanism; it does not discharge the burden of showing the mechanism is *used* — and several such equivalences fail that second test or haven't faced it.

## Claim A — Two substrates, two failure modes; humans lack the clean split

**This is the strongest claim in the cluster, but the steelman overreaches in three specific places.**

**1. The rank-collapse "spectral mechanism" premise is the architecture nobody deploys — and the steelman half-admits it, then uses it anyway.** Dong, Cordonnier & Loukas (2103.03404, ICML 2021) prove doubly-exponential convergence to rank-1 *for pure self-attention with no skip connections and no MLPs*. The same paper's whole point ("Attention is *Not* All You Need") is that **residual connections and MLPs prevent the collapse** — the authors' own path-decomposition shows the length-zero residual path preserves high-rank content, and MLPs slow convergence via their Lipschitz constant (confirmed via the authors' repo and follow-ups this session). Every deployed transformer has both. So presenting spectral collapse as a *parametric degradation law of a deployed transformer* inverts the paper: it is a theorem about why the toy architecture *can't* be deployed, not a failure mode of the ones that are. The honest version of the claim loses its crispest "provable mechanism" and is left with ordinary interference — which is not architecture-specific and not obviously a disanalogy.

**2. "Abrupt and complete" catastrophic interference is contested at LLM scale — arguably reversed.** McCloskey & Cohen (1989) and French (1999) describe small connectionist nets. But Ramasesh, Lewkowycz & Dyer ("Effect of scale on catastrophic forgetting," ICLR 2022, Google) find **large pretrained transformers are markedly *more* resistant to forgetting, and resistance improves systematically with scale** — the opposite of "abrupt and complete." There is countervailing evidence (Luo et al. 2308.08747 report forgetting *worsening* across 1–7B during continual fine-tuning), so the field is genuinely split. That split is the attack: the steelman cites the 1989/1999 "abrupt and complete loss" framing as if it transferred cleanly to LLMs, when the modern empirical picture is mixed-to-contradictory. You cannot simultaneously claim the mechanism is a *shared convergence* with CLS *and* that it manifests as clean catastrophic loss in the systems under discussion.

**3. Lost-in-the-Middle is substantially a calibratable positional-encoding artifact, not a "substrate occupancy law."** Liu et al. (2307.03172, TACL 2024) is real, but Yu et al., "Found in the Middle: Calibrating Positional Attention Bias Improves Long Context Utilization" (2406.16008, ACL 2024 Findings) show the U-shape is a **positional-attention bias that can be estimated and subtracted at inference time**, recovering up to ~10–15 points. If a training-free calibration removes much of it, then "information in the middle is *functionally starved by occupancy*" is overstated — a large part of it is a fixable property of the attention/positional-encoding implementation, i.e. a **measurement/implementation artifact**, not a degradation law of the KV substrate. Likewise, StreamingLLM's perplexity explosion (Xiao et al. 2309.17453) is a pathology of *one naive fixed-window eviction policy*; the same paper's attention-sink fix *removes the catastrophe*. A "failure mode" that a few retained tokens abolish is an engineering choice, not an intrinsic substrate law.

**Where A genuinely resists attack:** the *hard architectural gate* — weights frozen at inference, context discarded at session end — is real and does not have a biological homolog. Biological memory has no frozen-parameter/discarded-buffer wall; CLS (McClelland et al. 1995) is a continuous consolidation *gradient*. That structural asymmetry survives. What does *not* survive is the specific degradation-law framing (spectral collapse, clean catastrophic loss, occupancy-starvation) that the steelman uses to make the two-substrate story vivid — each of those is either the wrong architecture, contested at scale, or partly a calibratable artifact. **The defensible core is thinner than the steelman's version: "frozen weights + volatile buffer" is the disanalogy; the named failure laws are not load-bearing and are individually attackable.**

## Claim B — "Where is a fact stored?" may be ill-posed

**The evidence is real but the conclusion is scoped wider than the evidence licenses.**

**1. Hase (2301.04213, NeurIPS 2023) shows read-locus ≠ *edit*-locus — not that storage is ill-posed.** The verified finding (checked this session): causal-tracing localization is statistically uncorrelated with *which MLP layer is best to edit*. That is a dissociation between *where a fact is read in the forward pass* and *where an intervention most efficiently changes behavior*. In any distributed, redundant representation — biological or artificial — you would *expect* the cheapest write-point to differ from the read-locus; that does not make "where is it represented" ill-posed, it makes "where do I edit" a different, engineering question. The steelman's own phrasing ("read-locus ≠ write-locus, measured, reproducible") is the *narrow* true claim; "may not carve the system at a joint" is a philosophical escalation the Hase result does not itself establish. Note also Wang & Veitch, "Does Editing Provide Evidence for Localization?" (OpenReview, surfaced this session) — the follow-up literature is explicitly about disentangling these as *different measurements*, which cuts against treating either as dispositive about "storage."

**2. Distributed/non-localized storage is the *default expectation from neuroscience*, so the "disanalogy" partly dissolves.** The steelman concedes Lashley's engram search, then claims the LLM force is the *operational dissociation*. But Lashley, and the modern distributed-engram / redundant-ensemble literature, already say biological memory has no single address and that lesion (≈ "edit") effects dissociate from recording ("read") loci. If both substrates are distributed and both show read/write-locus dissociation, then **this is a point of *convergence*, not a break** — the steelman has it backwards for B relative to its own thesis. Degenerate knowledge neurons (Chen et al. 2402.13731, AAAI-2024 venue *still unconfirmed this session*) reinforce distribution/redundancy, which is exactly what biological memory also exhibits.

**3. Ripple-effect non-propagation (Cohen et al. 2307.12976, TACL 2024) is strong — but it indicts *editing methods*, not the storage concept.** That a ROME/MEMIT-style patch fails to propagate to entailed facts shows current *edit operators* are local and dumb; it does not show the fact was never coherently represented. A human "corrected" on one fact also routinely fails to propagate the correction to its inferential closure (belief-updating is notoriously non-monotonic). So non-propagation is at least as much a shared cognitive limitation as an LLM-specific storage artifact.

**Where B genuinely resists attack:** the *measured, reproducible* read/edit dissociation (Hase) plus non-unique degenerate encodings are a real, quantified property that makes the naive "one fact = one neuron address" thesis (strong-form Dai et al. 2104.08696) untenable — and Niu et al. (2405.02421, ICLR 2024 Spotlight, confirmed) independently show edit methods perturb syntax, not just facts. **What resists is the falsification of strong localism; what does *not* survive is the leap to "storage locus is ill-posed / doesn't carve a joint," which is a stronger metaphysical claim the cited results don't reach, and which — via the distributed-engram parallel — is a convergence rather than a disanalogy.**

## Claim C — Writable memory as an attack surface with no biological analogue

**This is the cluster's strongest *break*, and it mostly resists. The attackable part is the word "no analogue."**

**1. The false-memory literature is a closer analogue than the steelman allows.** The steelman dismisses Loftus-style misinformation and false-memory implantation as "probabilistic, reconstructive, not privileged writes." But the empirical record is stronger than that concession implies: Loftus & Pickrell's "lost in the mall" and, more damningly, Shaw & Porter (2015, *Psychological Science*) implanted *rich false memories of committing a crime* in ~70% of subjects through suggestive interviewing — durable, later-retrieved-*as-genuine*, and treated by the subject with the same authority as real autobiographical memory. That is precisely "an external agent, via presented input, installs a durable record the subject later retrieves as trusted ground truth." The mechanism differs (reconstructive encoding vs `insert()`), but the *threat-model shape* — untrusted external input → durable, trusted-on-recall memory → downstream behavior — has a real cognitive instance. "No biological analogue" is doing rhetorical work; the honest claim is "a *weaker, probabilistic, salience-gated* analogue exists, and the disanalogy is one of **reliability and directness**, not existence."

**2. MINJA/PoisonedRAG persistence "survives model updates" is partly an artifact of *how these systems are built*, not a substrate-deep property.** PoisonedRAG (Zou et al. 2402.07867; USENIX Security **2025** per authors' repo — the "2024" is the arXiv year) and MINJA (Dong et al. 2503.03704) are genuine existence proofs. But note the framing risk: the malicious record "surviving session resets and model updates" is a property of an *external vector store the engineer chose to make persistent and trusted*, not of the LLM. The exploit lives in the *retrieval-and-trust plumbing*, which is exactly the point — but it means C is a claim about *engineered RAG/agent architectures*, not about "LLM memory" as a natural kind. That is fine for the thesis, but it weakens any implicit "LLMs are intrinsically poisonable" reading: the attack surface is an *added affordance*, so the disanalogy is with a **design decision**, not with the model's learned parameters. (The steelman's parametric-poisoning cousin — training-data poisoning — *does* have partial biological analogues in developmental conditioning, which is why C wisely stays on the *inference-time write channel*.)

**Where C genuinely resists attack:** the specific conjunction — *externally writable at inference time + retrieved-and-acted-on with the same authority as internal knowledge + no salience/consolidation gate + attacker needs no privilege* — is not instantiated by biological memory. Even Shaw-style implantation requires sustained, high-effort, subject-mediated reconstructive encoding over repeated sessions; it is not a single `insert()` of a record retrieved verbatim at full trust. And the steelman's research-state observation is correct and checkable: there is no settled, standard write-trust/provenance primitive — the 2025–2026 literature is a detector arms race, not a solved problem. **C is the one claim where "convergence" is genuinely absent rather than merely shallow; the only correction is to downgrade "no analogue" to "no reliable, ungated, privileged-write analogue."**

## Claim D — Elegant equivalences may be re-description, not mechanism

**The general thesis is correct and well-supported; the single sharpest exhibit is overstated.**

**1. Heap et al. (2501.17727) does NOT show SAE features are artifacts — and the authors say so explicitly.** This is the most important correction in the whole cluster. The paper was **retitled** to "Automated Interpretability Metrics Do Not Distinguish Trained and Random Transformers," and the authors *explicitly state* they do **not** claim SAEs fail to capture information beyond what a random transformer yields — only that *aggregate auto-interpretability metrics* don't distinguish the two (confirmed via the arXiv abstract this session). So the steelman's use of it ("the single most damaging re-description result… 'the SAE found the model's concept' is not licensed") **overclaims the paper**. What is licensed: auto-interp *scores* are a bad null-model-free metric. What is *not* licensed: that SAE features are decoder-side artifacts. Using this paper as the knockout blow repeats exactly the "outrun the evidence" error the claim is warning against — a nice reflexive irony. The defensible D uses it only as "our headline metric fails a sanity check," which is real but much narrower.

**2. The attention↔Hopfield "identity ⇒ mere re-description" argument proves too much.** Ramsauer et al. (2008.02217, ICLR 2021, confirmed) is a genuine identity. But the steelman's inference — "an identity is a re-description, therefore silent on the realized algorithm" — would, applied consistently, dissolve most of theoretical physics and all of computational-equivalence results. An identity that shows attention *implements* the modern-Hopfield update *does* constrain the realized algorithm: it tells you the operation's fixed points, capacity, and retrieval dynamics are those of associative memory, which is a *mechanistic* fact about what the layer computes, not just a vocabulary swap. The correct, narrower attack is not "identities are re-descriptions" (false in general) but "**this** identity licenses claims about the *function computed*, and is silent only about *biological* associative recall (different substrate, different learning rule, no key/value projection)." The false-equivalence is specifically the *human/biological* leap, not the math.

**3. The "non-unique basis ⇒ not a mechanism" argument (feature-splitting/absorption, seed-dependence) is the strongest sub-point and largely survives.** The 2502.04878 / 2502.16681 / 2505.20254 preprints (all arXiv, not archival-venue-verified) genuinely show SAE decompositions are non-canonical and initialization-dependent — a chosen basis. That does undercut "the SAE reveals *the* model's concepts." But even here: non-uniqueness of a decomposition does not entail it's *not tracking real structure* (PCA is non-unique under rotation yet tracks variance). The honest claim is "non-canonical," not "not mechanistic."

**Where D genuinely resists attack:** the *meta*-thesis — an elegant equivalence raises a mechanism *hypothesis* and does not discharge the burden of showing the mechanism is *used* — is correct and important, and the SAE-metric sanity-check failure (properly scoped) plus the non-canonicity results are real support. **What resists is the general epistemic caution; what does *not* survive is (a) using Heap as proof that features are artifacts, and (b) the blanket "identities are mere re-description," which overgeneralizes past the actual biological false-equivalence.** The genuinely-open long-context-as-solvent question is correctly left open; "Found in the Middle" actually *strengthens* the openness by showing the U-shape is partly calibratable, so neither "solvent" nor "irreducible retrieval structure" is settled — the steelman gets this one right.

## Cross-cutting red-team verdicts

- **A**: real disanalogy = frozen-weights/volatile-buffer *gate*; the named degradation laws (spectral collapse, clean catastrophic loss, positional starvation) are each attackable — wrong architecture (rank collapse needs no-residual, no-MLP), contested at scale (forgetting), or calibratable artifact (Lost-in-the-Middle). **Downgrade from "two failure laws" to "one architectural gate."**
- **B**: falsifies *strong* localism (survives); "storage is ill-posed" and "disanalogy" *don't* survive — read/edit dissociation and distributed redundancy are shared with biological engrams, i.e. **convergence, not break**.
- **C**: strongest, mostly survives; only fix is "no *reliable, ungated, privileged-write* analogue" — Shaw & Porter (2015) is a real, if weaker, cognitive instance of the threat-model shape.
- **D**: meta-caution survives; **Heap et al. is materially overstated** (authors explicitly disclaim the artifact reading; paper retitled to a metrics claim); "identities are re-description" overgeneralizes and the false-equivalence is specifically the *biological* leap.
