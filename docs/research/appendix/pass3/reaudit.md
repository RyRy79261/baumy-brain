# Re-audit — framework-biased / wrongly-accepted / wrongly-dismissed + stones unturned

## T1-plural — Memory is plural (the systems partition: working/episodic/semantic/procedural)
### flagged decisions
- [framework-biased] Claim A verdict: the working/long-term + declarative/procedural/episodic/semantic partition is SHARED across cog-sci and AI cognitive architectures — 'weakened-but-stands (high)'.
    - The entire independence sub-debate is conducted on a deck-stacking choice of witness: CoALA — which everyone concedes is an ADMITTED import — is treated as THE representative of AI's memory partition. The strongest AI/computational case for genuine independent convergence, Complementary Learning Systems (McClelland, McNaughton & O'Reilly 1995), was never named in any pass. CLS derives the fast-hippocampal / slow-neocortical split (~episodic/semantic, ~fast/slow) from the math of catastrophic interference in connectionist nets, not from reading Tulving/Squire, and it is the actual bridge to modern AI (experience replay, continual learning, generative replay). Its omission is why the 'how many independent bases' count kept sliding (three -> one -> two) — the count silently depends on which AI instance you pick. The label survives, but its warranting rests on a biased evidence selection.
- [wrongly-accepted] Overstated-enrichment flag: 'triply-derived CONVERGENT / three INDEPENDENT bases / arrived at independently' escalated beyond the original 'SHARED'.
    - The flag correctly kills 'independent' FOR CoALA, but the down-correction to 'two bases (behavioral + lesion)' was accepted without testing the AI/computational leg against its best instance. With CLS in view, a genuinely engineering-native derivation of the fast/slow (and by extension episodic/semantic) split exists, so 'arrived at independently by a computational tradition' is defensible for at least one cut — the very residue the adjudicator itself called 'the only thing that could rescue any convergence reading'. The record accepted an under-count as settled.
- [framework-biased] Gate must-fix: recalibrate A to 'TWO independent evidentiary bases (behavioral-experimental + lesion neuroscience)', not 'one lineage's idealization'.
    - Directionally right (it resists the red team's total collapse) but leans the other way subtly: it treats lesion dissociations as theory-NEUTRAL physical discoveries. Which tasks are administered (mirror-drawing = 'procedural'), what counts as 'spared', and reading a deficit as a missing SYSTEM are all guided by the taxonomy under dispute — Milner interpreted H.M. through the memory framework of the day. Counting the lesion base as fully independent re-imports a pro-systems assumption. Honest position: 'partially theory-laden but methodologically distinct' — and, separately, the count is likely THREE once CLS is admitted, not two.
- [wrongly-accepted] Adversarial MISS 1: the adjudicator adopted a clean Ryle->Anderson genealogy AS FACT and mis-weighted A; reviewer demanded A be SPLIT (independence fails for working/long-term, partially survives for declarative/procedural).
    - The gate's minimization was accepted too readily. Anderson implementing procedural knowledge as Newell's production rules is a documented case of borrowing from a COMPUTATIONAL formalism, not from Tulving/Squire — and CLS supplies a second, cleaner AI/computational derivation the reviewer did not even have. The cumulative case that the declarative/procedural (and fast/slow) cut has real AI-internal warrant is stronger than 'open residue', so the refusal to split A is under-calibrated. The verdict need not flip to 'stands', but the confidence that independence is 'largely borrowing' is a thumb.
- [wrongly-accepted] Adversarial MISS 2: the adjudicator ENDORSED a red-team strawman — flattening Newell & Simon's 'Human Problem Solving' into 'a cognitive-psychology monograph' to route the AI branch downstream of psychology.
    - The clearest instance of our own thumb on the scale. Newell and Simon were founders of AI; production systems are a computational formalism; 'Human Problem Solving' is simultaneously an AI and a psychology work. Selectively labeling it 'cognitive psychology' to manufacture AI-dependence is exactly the framing the gate is supposed to catch — and it soft-pedaled the catch precisely because the framing supported the preferred borrowed-lineage conclusion. A strawman that flatters the reviewer's own verdict got a pass a symmetric one would not have.
- [framework-biased] The recurring gloss (steelman 'honest turn', gate, and defender) that 'coarse plurality is robust / unanimous under ALL camps — even Cowan keeps working/long-term, even the components camp keeps declarative/nondeclarative'.
    - This silently privileges the store ontology by counting descriptive dissociability as agreement about architectural PLURALITY. A strong distributed/single-system connectionist (the very Dunn & Kirsner / Van Orden / PDP tradition the record cites elsewhere) holds the dissociations are EMERGENT from one substrate — plural behaviorally, singular architecturally. The levels-of-processing / transfer-appropriate-processing tradition (Craik & Lockhart 1972; Morris et al. 1977) rejects that 'stores' are the right ontology at all. So 'every camp is plural' is true only if you pre-define plurality as descriptive dissociation; under an architectural reading a real tradition dissents. The unanimity gloss makes the plural core look safer than the philosophies of memory actually make it.
### stones unturned
- Complementary Learning Systems (McClelland, McNaughton & O'Reilly 1995, Psychological Review 102:419-457; updated Kumaran, Hassabis & McClelland 2016). The genuine COMPUTATIONAL derivation of a fast-hippocampal / slow-neocortical (~episodic/semantic, ~fast/slow) split from catastrophic interference in connectionist nets — arrived at from the math of learning, not borrowed from Tulving/Squire, and the actual mechanistic bridge to modern AI (experience replay in DQN, continual learning, generative replay). NONE of the passes named it. It is the strongest missing evidence on Claim A: it partially restores the 'independent convergence' the record collapsed, because the passes fixated on CoALA (an admitted import) as the sole AI witness.
- Constructive / generative episodic memory (Schacter & Addis 2007 constructive episodic simulation hypothesis; Bartlett 1932 reconstructive memory; Loftus misinformation work). Episodic memory as reconstruction and future-simulation rather than stored-trace retrieval. Bears on Claim B on an axis ORTHOGONAL to autonoetic consciousness — a retrieval-log is not a constructive recombination engine — so B can be defended without the contested autonoetic criterion, and this framing also challenges the very 'store-and-retrieve' ontology both AI agents and the systems partition assume.
- Levels-of-processing / transfer-appropriate processing (Craik & Lockhart 1972; Morris, Bransford & Franks 1977). The original PROCESS-view rival to the modal model: retention is a function of encoding depth and encoding-retrieval match, not of which store an item lands in. The passes cite Roediger's 'components' but never the tradition that most directly denies stores are the correct ontology — directly relevant to whether the repeated 'every camp is plural' claim is even true.
- The implicit/explicit (awareness) basis of the declarative/nondeclarative cut (Graf & Schacter 1985; Reber's implicit learning; Squire's own definition of nondeclarative as expression WITHOUT awareness). The passes treat declarative/procedural as a facts-vs-skills CONTENT distinction, but Squire's nondeclarative is criterially about absence of consciousness. Applied to AI this generalizes Claim B to the 'procedural' label: an agent's stored executable code/tools has nothing to do with non-conscious/implicit acquisition, so 'procedural memory' in CoALA may be an even looser analogy than 'episodic' — a symmetry the audit never drew.
- Engram / memory-trace neuroscience (Josselyn & Tonegawa 2020; optogenetic engram tagging and reactivation; Ramirez/Liu false-memory implantation). Modern cellular evidence on whether memories are discrete, physically localizable stored entities. Bears on the store-vs-process ontology at the mechanistic level; no pass touched the biology-of-the-trace literature that could ground (or undermine) 'stores' below the behavioral level.
- Systems-consolidation gradient theories (Standard Consolidation vs Multiple Trace Theory; Nadel & Moscovitch 1997; Winocur & Moscovitch). Episodic memories gradually 'semanticize' over time. If episodic->semantic is a temporal consolidation gradient, the two 'boxes' are endpoints of ONE process, not two stores — a distinct argument against a hard episodic/semantic border that the passes never raised (they used only Tulving's SPI dependency).
- Activity-silent / synaptic working memory and attention-based WM accounts beyond Cowan (Stokes 2015 'activity-silent' WM; Engle's WM-as-executive-attention; Mongillo synaptic-theory-of-WM). Further erodes a dedicated WM STORE from the neuroscience side — WM as a transient state of LTM synapses or as controlled attention, not a box. The passes leaned entirely on Cowan's activated-LTM and missed the activity-silent literature that pushes the same conclusion harder.
- Psychometric / individual-differences structure of memory (latent-factor work on whether episodic and semantic ability load on separable factors; g-vs-domain-specific memory factors). An entirely different, CORRELATIONAL evidentiary base for or against plurality — orthogonal to lesions and computational modeling — that neither pass invoked, and that could corroborate or undercut 'plural' independently of the store/process debate.
- Extended / distributed-cognition views of memory (Clark & Chalmers 1998 extended mind; transactive memory; Sparrow 'Google effect'). Whether 'memory' is even individual-brain-bounded. Directly relevant to AI because an agent's external store is literally an extended-memory case — the 'systems partition' framing forecloses this competing philosophy without argument.
- Non-storage / predictive-processing and free-energy framings of memory (memory as parameters of a generative model / as inference). Dissolves the whole store taxonomy into a single inference process — the most radical competitor to 'memory is plural at the level of discrete stores', and completely absent from all passes.

## T2-encoding — "Encoding is distillation, never raw storage"
### flagged decisions
- [framework-biased] Claim A verdict (stores transformed traces, never the raw stream) — adjudicated weakened-but-stands/high, gate-corrected to medium + Generative Agents struck + MTT-attack re-weighted down.
    - The surviving-form rescue silently adopts the reconstructive/abstractionist definition of 'trace' — under which ANY stored representation counts as 'transformed.' That definition is one school's. The instance/multiple-trace school (Hintzman MINERVA-2 1986; Nosofsky GCM) holds the opposite: encoding retains a separate near-raw trace per experience and abstraction is computed at RETRIEVAL, so under that school 'encoding is distillation' is simply false, not weakened. Both passes treated the detail-persistence evidence (Brady, MTT) only as scattered 'attacks' and never gave the instance-model school standing as a coherent rival philosophy. The high→medium fix corrects the confidence-laundering but the retained 'deep convergence — genuine' and the very framing 'encoding IS distillation, NEVER raw' still presuppose the abstractionist answer to a question memory science treats as open (is abstraction an encode-time or retrieve-time operation?).
- [framework-biased] Strawman flag on A: red team 'conflates high-fidelity detail persists (Brady 2008, MTT) with the raw stream persists.'
    - This flag is the confidence-laundering device the Pass-2 attacker identified. Whether Brady's persisted ITEM-SPECIFIC PERCEPTUAL detail (pose/exemplar/state) counts as 'transformed' or 'raw' depends entirely on which school defines 'trace' — under the abstractionist/reconstructive school it is 'transformed'; under the instance-model school it is precisely the near-raw stored content. The flag deployed the abstractionist definition to quarantine Brady while preserving strong-A credit, i.e. it makes A unfalsifiable and then treats that as a defense. The gate's medium downgrade partially neutralizes it, but the flag itself silently depends on choosing one school.

The resolution — retreat 'convergence' to FUNCTIONAL convergence under capacity pressure — is near-analytic (any bounded channel compresses), and paying it should force the SAME downgrade of A's and C's 'deep/genuine convergence' language that D conceded. The record parked this in an open question but left A's 'convergence — genuine' and C's 'directly load-bearing convergence' prose intact. So the dissolution's price was charged to D only, not propagated to A/C — the cluster keeps its strong convergence rhetoric after adopting the weak reading that dissolves the contradiction. That is our thumb.
- [wrongly-accepted] Pass-2 note: using Fellegi-Sunter's cost-neutrality to attack B overshoots B's domain-scoped (high-merge-cost regime) claim — a red-team strawman.

An attack was left partly over-credited.
### stones unturned
- Instance/exemplar models as a RIVAL PHILOSOPHY, not an attack: Hintzman's MINERVA-2 (1986, Psych Rev 93:411) holds that each experience lays down a SEPARATE near-raw multi-attribute trace and that abstraction/gist is computed AT RETRIEVAL (the summed 'echo'), never stored at encoding. Under this well-respected school 'encoding is distillation' is simply false — distillation is a read-time operation. No pass named it in T2; the entire cluster presupposes the abstractionist answer to the genuinely-open question 'is abstraction an encode-time or a retrieve-time process?'
- Rate-distortion / resource-rational memory (Bhui, Lai & Gershman 2021; efficient-coding, rational-inattention): the rigorous formalization of 'distillation' as lossy compression under an explicit distortion function. This is exactly the falsifiable boundary between 'transformed' and 'raw' the record's open questions kept begging for — the distortion metric IS the operationalization — AND it cuts both ways: it shows the cross-field 'convergence' may be near-analytic (any capacity-bounded channel compresses optimally), giving the vacuity charge formal teeth. Neither pass reached for it.
- AI-side counter-DATA to 'operational substrate is never raw': raw-passage/chunk RAG frequently OUTPERFORMS extracted-triple/KG memory; production vector DBs store embeddings of verbatim chunks and retrieve the raw text; long-context models operate over raw tokens. The passes leaned only on distillation-friendly architectures (Generative-Agents reflections, KG triples) and never confronted the dominant real-world AI memory pattern, which is operational storage of (an encoding of) the raw stream.
- Hippocampal indexing theory (Teyler & DiScenna 1986) and sparse distributed memory (Kanerva): the hippocampus stores a POINTER/index that REINSTATES a distributed neocortical pattern — so 'what is stored' is an index and the reinstated content is the near-original cortical pattern, which reframes the raw-vs-distilled binary. Teyler-DiScenna was named-but-undeveloped in the red team; the indexing school was never engaged as an alternative to 'stored derivative.'
- Engram / optogenetic-tagging neuroscience (Josselyn & Frankland; Tonegawa silent engrams): the most direct modern cellular evidence about what a trace IS. Silent engrams show information PERSISTS but is inaccessible — complicating both 'distilled/discarded at write time' and 'raw is a cold archive.' Entirely absent from every pass.
- Constructive episodic simulation / reconstruction-at-retrieval (Schacter & Addis 2007; Nader reconsolidation): shifts the distillation locus to retrieval and shares machinery with imagination, questioning whether 'encoding' is even the right unit for the thesis. The passes cited Bartlett/reconstruction one-directionally but never the modern constructive-simulation framework that relocates the action to read time.
- Bjork & Bjork's New Theory of Disuse (storage strength vs retrieval strength, 1992): a two-factor framework offering the intrinsic-strength dimension that Claim C's 'purely relational, never intrinsic' TAP reading flatly denies — a THIRD option beyond LoP and TAP directly on-point for C, never named.
- The capacity premise itself is unexamined: the core inference is 'bounded capacity ⇒ must distill at encoding,' but human storage estimates are enormous (Landauer 1986 ~10^9 bits; Brady 2008's own scale) and the binding constraint may be interference/retrieval, not storage. If storage is not the bottleneck, the cluster's foundational 'why' collapses at the root — no pass questioned whether biological memory is even capacity-bound in the relevant sense.
- Fuzzy-trace theory is DUAL-storage, not distillation-only: the steelman cited it FOR distillation ('gist durable, verbatim decays'), but its actual claim is that verbatim AND gist are stored in PARALLEL and independently — i.e. the system DOES lay down a near-verbatim trace. A partly-anti-thesis source was used one-directionally as pro-thesis evidence and no pass noticed.
- HSAM / eidetic individual differences (LePort et al. 2012, highly superior autobiographical memory): near-verbatim autobiographical retention as direct data on whether 'never raw' is a hard architectural constraint or merely a typical-case tendency with a heavy-tailed distribution. Unconsidered.
- Working memory as a bounded operational store of near-RAW content (Baddeley): a capacity-bounded system whose operational substrate is near-verbatim, standing as a live counterexample to the 'capacity-bounded ⇒ distill' inference. The passes waved this past as 'sensory buffers, briefly' and never engaged WM proper.
- Algorithmic-information / compression-as-prediction framing (MDL; Hutter): would give the red team's vacuity charge formal grounding — 'distillation' is near-definitional of any predictive learner, making the cross-field 'convergence' analytic rather than an empirical discovery. The vacuity charge was leveled rhetorically but never formalized, leaving it easy to wave away.

## T3-weighting
### flagged decisions
- [framework-biased] Claim A — 3-factor weighting is a shared backbone converging with ACT-R activation (overall verdict).
    - Pass-2's provenance fix is correct, but the whole convergence apparatus silently adopts the rational/adaptive school (ACT-R) as THE human anchor a system can 'converge on.' That framing presupposes memory has a factorized per-item weighting to begin with. Rival ontologies — instance/exemplar and multiple-trace models (Hintzman's MINERVA-2, Logan's instance theory) — carry NO importance weight at all; retrieval is an emergent echo over stored traces. Against that school the claim doesn't 'weaken,' it dissolves (there is nothing to factor). No pass admitted a third pole, so the verdict lives entirely inside an ACT-R-vs-engineering-knob dichotomy that is itself a thumb on the scale.

    - The corrections are good, but the residual 'principled/Bayes-diagnosticity' reading privileges Anderson & Milson's rational-analysis interpretation of the fan effect over live competitors that give no normative-quantity reading: interference-resistance (Bunting, Conway & Heitz 2004) and inhibition/suppression — a shift Anderson & Reder themselves made. Whether hub down-weighting is 'the signature of correct normalization' or merely a measured cost depends on which fan-effect theory you pick; the verdict quietly picks the diagnosticity one.

Two formulas having the same shape is 'same behavior'; 'shared normative quantity' is 'same reason' — exactly the inference the red team correctly attacked elsewhere, quietly reinstated here because it flatters the convergence narrative.
- [wrongly-dismissed] Red-team B3: degree-normalization suppresses a correct central entity as much as an uninformative one.

- [framework-biased] Claim C — retrievability tracks environmental need-probability; near-optimal → resource-rational (overall verdict).
    - The push toward 'survives' rehabilitates the rational-analysis reading. The attacks that genuinely land — the three corpora are one Zipfian statistical family, and the just-so free-knobs charge (analyst picks environment, corpus, cost, objective; resource-rationality adds a free cost function) — indict the whole rational-analysis program, not just the 'near-optimal' gloss. Upgrading C's standing on the strength of an on-domain citation while treating those program-level attacks as mere 'nicks' tilts toward the school under audit.
- [wrongly-accepted] Claim C: Averell & Heathcote (2011) added as the on-domain individual-level result that 'partially answers C's open question in C's favor.'
    - Verified live: exponential BEST fit the individual RAW data under both explicit and implicit instructions; a power function won ONLY after complexity-penalized Bayesian model selection (which accounts for functions mimicking each other). Deploying it as 'individual-level correspondence in C's favor' over-reads a genuinely split result — the raw-data exponential win is the anti-power-law (anti-rational-form) reading, and it got buried. Even-handedly, A&H 2011 partly rescues the correspondence AND partly undercuts the specific power-law form; only the favorable half was credited.
- [framework-biased] Claim D sub-claim: importance UNIQUELY lacks a normative anchor, unlike recency and relevance.
    - Two silent tilts. (1) Recency and relevance are 'anchored' ONLY inside rational analysis / ACT-R — remove that school and all three factors are equally un-anchored, so the asymmetry is school-relative, not a fact about memory. (2) The audit scoped 'importance' to 2023–24 LLM systems plus ACT-R and never consulted the affective-neuroscience literature (McGaugh: emotional arousal → epinephrine/glucocorticoid → basolateral-amygdala-modulated consolidation, so memory strength tracks emotional significance), nor value-based memory (reward/expected-utility prioritization), nor prediction-error/novelty gating. Those ARE candidate normative anchors for importance. D's 'importance is uniquely free-floating' is partly an artifact of the sources it chose to inspect.

    - D3 was over-dismissed. Beneath the deflation it names the exact framework bias: demanding 'shared edge-weight SEMANTICS' presupposes memory weights must MEAN a single quantity — a rational-analysis assumption that an instance-theory or pure-engineering view rejects outright (a weight can be a bare tuning knob with no referent). Treating that as a mere 'so what' let the audit keep its premise (that importance ought to have a shared meaning) unexamined.
- [framework-biased] Under-credited strength: 'convergence is the null in a tiny design space' was accepted without checking that the 2023 LLM-retrieval norm was plain top-k cosine (divergence was live).
    - This restoration tilts back toward the rational-rediscovery narrative on the strength of an uncited empirical claim about 2023 norms. ### stones unturned
- Instance / multiple-trace / exemplar models (Hintzman's MINERVA-2; Logan's instance theory; Shiffrin & Steyvers' REM). A whole rival ontology in which there is NO per-item importance weight at all — retrieval is an emergent 'echo' or global match over independently stored traces. Against this school 'recency × importance × relevance' is a category mistake, not a weakened-but-standing claim. No pass admitted it as a third pole to ACT-R and 'engineering knob'; its existence is what makes the topic's framing biased.
- Global-matching / interference family as a standalone weighting philosophy (SAM — Raaijmakers & Shiffrin; TODAM; REM). The passes cited SAM/interference only as 'competitors to the fan effect,' never as an alternative account of how memories are weighted (cue-context matching + interference, no diagnosticity-scored activation). This directly bears on Claim B: if the fan effect is interference/inhibition (Bunting 2004; Anderson & Reder's own migration), the 'principled Bayes-diagnosticity' reading loses its privileged status.
- Bjork's New Theory of Disuse — two independent strengths (storage strength vs retrieval strength) with opposite update dynamics. A direct competitor to the single-composite-score assumption: recency and importance would be two orthogonal quantities, not co-multiplied factors, and retrieval itself modifies weights. The entire topic assumes a one-dimensional retrieval score; this two-factor structural theory was never considered.
- Affective-salience neuroscience of importance (McGaugh: emotional arousal → epinephrine + glucocorticoids → basolateral-amygdala modulation of consolidation, so memory strength reflects emotional significance; plus flashbulb-memory and arousal-enhanced-consolidation work). This is a mechanistic, arguably normative BIOLOGICAL account of exactly the quantity Claim D declares 'unanchored.' Its total absence is why D's 'importance uniquely free-floating' reads as an artifact of the sources chosen (LLM systems + ACT-R only).
- Complementary Learning Systems and consolidation (McClelland, McNaughton & O'Reilly 1995; Kumaran, Hassabis & McClelland 2016), plus prioritized replay. Here 'importance/weighting' is about what gets consolidated and replayed, governed by prediction error and novelty — a structurally different theory than a static retrieval-time score. Its engineering cousin, prioritized experience replay (Schaul et al. 2016) with TD-error as the importance signal, is a concrete normative importance metric neither the passes nor the surveyed LLM systems mentioned.
- Value-based and reward-modulated memory (Adcock et al.; Shohamy/Braun; the reward-prediction and motivated-memory literature). This supplies importance with a decision-theoretic anchor — expected utility / reward — which is precisely the 'one latent target' the red team hypothesized for D's four proxies but no pass connected to the actual experimental literature. It is the missing empirical test of D's under-unification question.
- Predictive-processing / surprise-as-importance (Friston free-energy; Lisman & Grace hippocampal–VTA novelty gating; information-gain/Bayesian-surprise encoding). Provides a candidate normative derivation for importance (encode in proportion to prediction error / information gain) that would partially refute D's 'no normative anchor for importance,' and was never surfaced.
- Reconstructive and gist/verbatim views (Bartlett; Reyna & Brainerd fuzzy-trace theory; Loftus reconsolidation). Memory is reconstructed at retrieval, not read from a fixed weighted store. This undercuts the shared premise of the whole topic — that there exist stable 'edge weights' whose semantics could even be compared across systems.
- Optimal caching / eviction theory as an ALTERNATIVE optimality frame (Belady's optimal algorithm; LRU/LFU; rate-distortion / lossy-compression accounts of memory). The passes tied 'optimality' exclusively to Anderson's need-probability. Cache-replacement theory offers an independent, arguably cleaner normative justification for recency/frequency weighting — with its own proofs and no Zipfian-corpus dependence — that would let the recency/frequency factors be 'anchored' without buying into rational analysis at all. Weighing it would have relieved the framework dependence flagged throughout.
- Retrieval-induced forgetting and inhibitory retrieval dynamics (Anderson, Bjork & Bjork). Accessing one item actively SUPPRESSES its relatives — an inhibitory, competition-based weighting dynamic absent from the additive/multiplicative recency×importance×relevance model, and a structural phenomenon the whole topic's read-only 'scoring' framing cannot represent.
- Whether 'importance' is even empirically separable from recency×frequency×attention. A body of work argues apparent salience effects reduce to exposure/attention/frequency. If so, D's premise — that importance is a genuine distinct third factor needing its own theory — could partly collapse. No pass tested the separability that its own claim presupposes.
- Ecological/non-linguistic environments for the need-probability correspondence (spatial and motor memory; optimal-foraging and animal memory literatures). The Zipfian same-family critique of Claim C was raised but never answered by looking outside heavy-tailed human text — the one body of evidence that could actually separate 'adaptation to environmental statistics' from 'both curves are power-law because both are aggregated / both are Zipfian.'

- [framework-biased] The overarching binary framing — 'associative completion' vs 'address lookup' — as the exhaustive space of memory philosophies
    - This is the record's largest thumb on the scale. ### stones unturned
- Global-matching / signal-detection memory models as a THIRD philosophy neither pass considered: SAM (Raaijmakers & Shiffrin, Psych Review 1981), REM (Shiffrin & Steyvers, Psychonomic Bull. & Rev. 1997), TODAM (Murdock 1982), and MINERVA 2 (Hintzman, Psych Review 1986, 93:411–428). These treat retrieval as parallel similarity-weighted global familiarity / likelihood-ratio evidence accumulation — not graph traversal AND not address lookup. They quantitatively fit the mirror effect, list-strength effect, and ROC slopes, data the completion thesis never engages. Tellingly, Hintzman appears in our record only as a Tulving–Wiseman critic; his MINERVA 2 — a competing constructive model — was never seated.
- Dual-process recognition (recollection vs familiarity; Yonelinas 2002; Remember/Know; process-dissociation). Retrieval decomposes into a fast scalar-like familiarity signal PLUS a slower recollective/associative route. This is direct evidence that at least one retrieval route behaves like the 'scalar strength' the thesis dismisses as the strawman null — with strong ROC and process-dissociation support the passes never weighed.
- Complementary Learning Systems (McClelland, McNaughton & O'Reilly, Psych Review 1995, 102:419–457) plus hippocampal indexing theory (Teyler & DiScenna 1986). The neuro architecture posits TWO structurally different stores — a sparse pattern-separated hippocampal INDEX (an address-like pointer to neocortical reinstatement) alongside distributed neocortex. This arguably supports completion and indexing COEXISTING, directly undercutting the either/or framing the whole cluster assumes.
- Rational / Bayesian analysis of memory (Anderson & Schooler, Psych Science 1991, 2:396–408; ACT-R activation). Memory availability tracks environmental need-probability (frequency, recency, spacing). This reframes retrieval 'structure' as adaptation to environmental statistics — an orthogonal explanatory level — and notably gives a principled account of the recency-composition step that owes nothing to associative completion.
- Reconstructive / generative memory (Bartlett 1932; schema theory; Schacter & Addis constructive-episodic-simulation). Retrieval can produce content never stored (DRM false memories, gist intrusions). MINERVA 2's prototype extraction and Hopfield's spurious/metastable states are the computational echoes. 'Completion vs lookup' has no room for retrieval-as-fabrication, a large empirical body the passes skipped.
- Temporal-context models (TCM; Howard & Kahana 2002) and the contiguity/lag-recency structure of episodic recall. Here the CUE is a drifting temporal-context vector, not content — a different answer to 'what addresses memory' than content-addressable completion, and the model that actually explains contiguity data. Directly relevant to the pipeline's unexamined 'recency-composed' retrieval step.
- Retrieval-induced forgetting and inhibitory memory structure (Anderson, Bjork & Bjork 1994). Retrieval is competitive and actively SUPPRESSES rivals. Pure spreading-activation / energy-descent predicts facilitation, not inhibition, so this is a direct empirical challenge to Claim A's spreading-activation pillar that no pass surfaced.
- Information theory / rate-distortion as its own competing account, not a 'decorative frame.' The red team dismissed the info-theoretic explanation of BM25>dense as 'no memory theory needed,' but memory-as-lossy-channel (retrieval quality bounded by encoder rate-distortion) is a substantive structural theory of memory that deserved seating as a rival rather than being waved off.
- Whether external retrieval structure matters at all under long context / strong parametric memory. The passes argued benchmarks but never considered the live 'long-context dissolves the retrieval-structure question' body of work — i.e., an empirical challenge to the entire retrieval-centric framing, and to whether graph/hierarchy/flat distinctions persist as context windows grow.
- No capacity, forgetting, or cost axis was ever weighed. Competing philosophies of memory differ sharply on forgetting-as-feature (Ebbinghaus curves; adaptive/Bayesian forgetting) and capacity limits. Every claim was scored on retrieval accuracy alone, silently privileging schools that optimize recall over schools for which selective forgetting IS the structural point.

The steelman/red-team/adjudication were rigorous WITHIN the consolidation-transformation school — verifying citations, splitting sufficiency from necessity, catching the RAPTOR inconsistency — but the debate was framed as 'defend or attack CLS/transformation,' never 'weigh CLS against its live competitors.' ### stones unturned
- Contextual binding theory (Yonelinas, Ranganath, Ekstrom & Wiltgen 2019, Nature Reviews Neuroscience, 'systems consolidation reconsidered') — a direct rival that attributes graded retrograde amnesia to contextual INTERFERENCE, not time-dependent fast->slow transfer, holds the hippocampus is permanently required, and REINTERPRETS the sleep benefit as reduced contextual interference rather than gist-abstraction. It attacks the cluster's core premise, not its edges, and no pass mentioned it.
- Fuzzy-Trace Theory (Reyna & Brainerd) — the cluster's own 'gist vs verbatim' vocabulary belongs to FTT, which holds the two representations are extracted IN PARALLEL AT ENCODING, not manufactured by an offline consolidation pass. This is a standing dissolution of Claim B's 'the offline pass is WHERE compression-into-schema happens'; its absence let Claim B survive an attack that was never posed.
- Reconsolidation (Nader, Schafe & LeDoux 2000, Nature) — retrieval destabilizes and re-stores memory, so memory is not write-once and the fast->slow 'transfer' pipeline is not directional in the way Claim A's replay-interleaving mechanism assumes. The passes gestured at MTT trace-multiplication but never engaged this larger, more mechanistically concrete body of data.
- Engram / silent-engram causal evidence (Kitamura et al. 2017, Science; 'Silent memory engrams,' PNAS 2017) — cortical memory engrams are generated on DAY ONE but remain silent and mature over ~2 weeks. This is the strongest modern causal neuroscience on consolidation and it complicates 'slow cortex gradually builds up via replay-interleaving,' yet it is entirely absent from all passes.
- Synaptic Homeostasis Hypothesis / SHY (Tononi & Cirelli) — the principal rival to the active-systems (replay-strengthens) account the cluster assumes: sleep NET-DOWNSCALES/renormalizes synapses, so 'shared structure survives while idiosyncratic detail decays' could be a byproduct of global downscaling, not targeted iOtA replay. The passes adopted active-systems consolidation without acknowledging its main competitor.
- Efficient-coding / rate-distortion / resource-rational memory (e.g. Gershman/Bhui/Nagy-line lossy-compression and resource-rational forgetting accounts) — offers a NORMATIVE derivation of 'compress to gist' from information theory that needs neither the interference argument nor a fast/slow split. It would let one ask whether CLS's interference rationale is THE reason for gist or merely ONE reason among competing 'why's — a question none of the passes framed.
- Retrieval-based learning / the testing effect and desirable difficulties (Roediger & Karpicke; Bjork) — an entire behavioral tradition in which durable memory is built by RETRIEVAL PRACTICE at test time, not by offline replay/consolidation. It competes head-on with Claim B's 'the offline pass is where it happens,' and was never considered.
- The 'no-consolidation' AI counter-philosophy (large-context models / verbatim RAG) — the passes only tested pro-consolidation AI analogues (RAPTOR, sleep-time compute) and never confronted the strongest engineering rival: systems that deliberately do NOT consolidate, keeping everything verbatim and letting attention retrieve. Its omission pre-loads the AI comparison toward the cluster's thesis.
- Human-vs-rodent evidence asymmetry — nearly all the causal consolidation evidence (Tse schemas, engram optogenetics, replay) is rodent, while human time-graded retrograde amnesia after selective hippocampal damage is reportedly rare. No pass raised the standard skeptic's question of whether the cross-species generalization to a 'human/AI memory architecture' actually holds.
- Generative-model / predictive-processing accounts of memory (memory as inference in a generative model; Friston/Barron-line) — appeared only as the dismissed 'generative replay crack,' never engaged as a competing STRUCTURAL philosophy in which the storage-vs-transform dichotomy the whole cluster rests on is itself the wrong frame.

### stones unturned
- Complementary Learning Systems (McClelland, McNaughton & O'Reilly 1995, Psych Review 102:419-457) — the canonical biological answer to 'how do you update memory without catastrophic interference': a fast, sparse, pattern-separated hippocampal store plus a slow, interleaved neocortical store. This two-rate architecture is THE memory-updating framework in neuroscience and it directly reframes the whole T6 question (update-fast-then-consolidate-slow, not reconcile-vs-append) — yet no pass cited it. It also supplies the mechanistic bridge C was said to lack: catastrophic collapse under sequential editing IS catastrophic interference, the exact failure CLS was invented to explain.
- Multiple / Competitive Trace Theory (Nadel & Moscovitch 1997, Curr Opin Neurobiol 7:217-227) — the neuroscience camp in which each retrieval lays down a NEW trace rather than modifying the old, i.e. accretion/coexistence rather than reconsolidation-overwrite. This is a direct biological instantiation of the append philosophy and a live rival to the reconsolidation story T6 selected as 'the' human analogue; it is itself contested ('Has multiple trace theory been refuted?', Hippocampus 2019). The passes debated reconsolidation's boundary conditions but never acknowledged the competing consolidation theory.
- Conflict-free replicated data types / merge-based conflict resolution (Shapiro, Preguica, Baquero & Zawirski 2011) — the passes' only systems counterexample was single-writer event-sourcing/CQRS. Multi-writer concurrent updating without any single supersession authority (last-write-wins vs merge/join semilattice semantics) is an entire design axis for 'updating' that maps onto a house-of-many-speakers memory and was never considered. Whether contradictory facts are merged, ranked, or superseded is exactly the open design question, and CRDT theory is the field that has formal results on it.
- Rational-analysis / activation-decay memory (Anderson & Schooler 1991, Psych Science 2:396-408; ACT-R) — memory as base-level activation that decays to match environmental need-probability, with no explicit DELETE and 'forgetting' as adaptive down-weighting rather than invalidation. A concrete rival to the discrete ADD/UPDATE/DELETE/NOOP operator set the cluster treats as inevitable; it says the right update primitive is graded reweighting, not typed supersession.
- Graded / Bayesian belief updating and predictive-coding — the cluster is entirely discrete-operator (a fact is current or superseded). A probabilistic memory holds every fact with a continuous confidence updated by evidence, and prediction-error minimization is the update rule. This dissolves 'supersede vs append' into 'reweight,' and no pass weighed continuous-confidence memory as a competing structure — even though the steelman's own 'prediction-error-gated reconsolidation' gesture points straight at it.
- Reconstructive / schema memory (Bartlett 1932, 'Remembering') — the anti-storage philosophy: there are no stored 'facts' to supersede at all; recall reconstructs from schemas, so 'updating a triple' is the wrong ontology from the start. This undercuts the entire store-a-{subject,predicate,object}-row-and-reconcile-it frame at its root and was never raised as a rival to the record-based assumption shared by all four claims.
- Catastrophic interference lineage and the stability-plasticity dilemma (McCloskey & Cohen 1989; French 1999; Grossberg's ART) — the connectionist ancestor of Claim C's sequential-editing collapse and the unifying frame that links C (machine) to reconsolidation (biology) under one problem: how to add new knowledge without erasing old. McCloskey appears in the record only for the misinformation effect; his catastrophic-interference work, which is the actual theoretical home of the collapse phenomenon, is absent.
- The empirical stakes of reconcile-at-read vs reconcile-at-write were named as an open question but never operationalized against any body of retrieval-augmentation ablation data. No pass consulted the RAG/long-context conflict-resolution literature on whether surfacing both contradictory facts vs pre-resolving them changes downstream answer accuracy — so the design question the whole topic pivots on was left as a purely representational debate with the empirical column blank.

- Memory reconsolidation (Nader, Schafe & LeDoux 2000, Nature 406:722-726; verified). A biologically real 'rewrite-on-retrieval' mechanism: recalled memories become labile and are re-stored, updatable during the window. Bears on Claim C ('no biological rewrite channel / gate by construction') and Claim A (the 'frozen store' framing) — biology's consolidated store is NOT frozen; it is reopened by recall. Not considered by any pass.
- One-trial / single-exposure durable learning — conditioned taste aversion (Garcia effect), single-event trauma, flashbulb memories. Direct counterexamples to Claim C's 'no verbatim single-exposure durable write exists' and to Claim A's 'time-gated gradual consolidation only.' They are salience-gated (so C's attacker-arbitrary security core may still stand) but the passes never tested whether single-shot biological writes exist at all.
- The functionalist / Marr's-levels objection to the entire comparison frame. Claims A and B assert SUBSTRATE-level disanalogies (two physical substrates; storage locus). Under computational-level functionalism these are implementation details that are irrelevant to a memory-function comparison. The record never flags that its substrate-disanalogy verdicts are only interesting if one rejects functionalism — a silent framework dependence spanning A and B.
- The reconstructive / source-monitoring school of human memory (Bartlett 1932; Loftus; Schacter's 'seven sins'; Marcia Johnson's source-monitoring framework). Only the narrow Shaw&Porter false-crime study was engaged. The broader reconstructive tradition holds human memory has no integrity guarantee and routinely retrieves suggested/confabulated content as trusted first-person truth — which would recast Claim C's disanalogy as quantitative (reliability/gating), not categorical, and was never weighed.
- Lesion-dissociation neuroscience (patient H.M.; anterograde amnesia) as a rival to the CLS-gradient model chosen for Claim A. It supplies a near-categorical biological encoding 'gate,' contradicting the record's assertion that biology has no frozen-store/blocked-write wall. The passes adopted CLS-gradient without weighing the dissociation evidence.
- Working-memory theory proper (Baddeley & Hitch; Cowan's embedded-processes; capacity ~4 chunks). Claim A dismisses 'context = working memory' as the weakest point of the analogy but never engages the actual WM literature — capacity limits, chunking, attentional gating — which could either sharpen or soften the KV-cache/context comparison. Engaged only rhetorically.
- Predictive-processing / generative-model accounts of memory (memory as reconstruction from a generative model rather than retrieval from a store; Hemmer & Steyvers; Bayesian reconstruction). Under this school 'where is the fact stored' (Claim B) is ill-posed for HUMANS too and memory is inherently generative — which would make B a convergence and is orthogonal to the connectionist-vs-symbolic axis the passes used.
- Training-data poisoning / sleeper-agent parametric attacks as the parametric COUSIN of Claim C. The steelman deliberately scoped C to the inference-time write channel and set parametric poisoning aside as having 'partial biological analogues in developmental conditioning' — but never actually adjudicated that comparison, which is where the closest biological analogue (early-life conditioning, imprinting) actually lives.
- Human semantic-vs-episodic memory update asymmetries and belief-updating literature (Tulving; motivated reasoning; the continued-influence effect). Claim B's ripple-edit non-propagation was compared to human failure-to-propagate corrections only in passing; the continued-influence-effect literature is a quantified human parallel that neither confirms nor breaks the analogy but was never brought in.
