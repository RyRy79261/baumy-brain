# Framework champions — bias verdicts

## Multi-store / modal model — **mildly-underweighted**
### under-weighted
- T1-C [survives/high]: 'whether a distinct short-term store exists vs activated LTM is itself contested in psychology' — the discrete-store commitment demoted to a coin-flip. → The modal model does not treat separate stores as a live tie; it holds they are the DEFAULT reading forced by convergent classic evidence: Sperling's sensory register, the primacy/recency serial-position curve, and Glanzer & Cunitz (1966) selectively abolishing recency (STS) with a filler delay while leaving primacy (LTS) intact. That is a within-paradigm double dissociation. Rating the store ontology 'contested/survives-high' silently re-baselines Cowan's embedded-processes / activated-LTM view as co-equal when the modal proponent holds the burden runs the other way.
- T5 consolidation cluster (two-timescale fast hippocampal + slow neocortex, CLS) [weakened-but-stands/high]. → This is a direct vindication of the modal architecture, not a separate story: a fast store and a slow store with a control process (rehearsal/consolidation/replay) transferring content between them IS the sensory->STS->LTS transfer diagram instantiated in neuroscience and ML. The corpus credits it to 'CLS / catastrophic-interference solution' and never books it as evidence that boxes-and-arrows-with-transfer is the winning design, so the framework's central claim goes uncredited where the corpus's own strongest cluster confirms it.
- T8-A [weakened-but-stands/high]: LLM memory 'bifurcated into two substrates (parametric weights vs non-parametric KV-cache/context)' framed as a DISANALOGY because 'humans have no clean parametric/non-parametric split.' → From the modal lens this is boxes-and-arrows realized in silicon: a volatile short-term buffer (context/KV window, capacity-bounded, eviction-governed) versus a durable long-term store (weights). The human 'clean split' the corpus says is missing is exactly the STS/LTS distinction the modal model posits. The corpus imports T1-C's demotion of the human store-split to convert a striking cross-substrate CONFIRMATION of two-store architecture into a disanalogy.
### wrongful dismissals
- T1-C accepting the 'activated LTM / embedded-processes / SPI continuum' as strong enough to leave the distinct-STS claim 'contested (survives/high).' → The neuropsychological double dissociation was set aside as merely 'one camp.' Revive it: Shallice & Warrington (1970, patient KF) show impaired auditory-verbal STS with intact LTS, the mirror image of amnesic patients (H.M./K.C.) with intact STS and abolished LTS. A double dissociation is the strongest single-inference tool cognitive neuropsychology has for separable systems, and it is precisely what a continuum/single-store account struggles to produce. Treating this as symmetric with 'it might all be activated LTM' understates a genuine asymmetry in the evidence.
- T8-A's dismissal 'humans have no clean parametric/non-parametric split' used to break the two-substrate parallel. → This is the T1-C thumb applied a second time. The STS/LTS distinction — bounded, rapidly-decaying, rehearsal-maintained buffer vs. slow, high-capacity, structurally-consolidated store — IS a volatile/durable split with different governing laws (time/displacement vs. interference/consolidation). The disanalogy is overstated; at minimum the human side supplies a 'clean split' the corpus denies it has, so the LLM bifurcation should count as convergence-on-two-stores at the architectural tier, with the divergence confined to the degradation LAWS rather than the presence of the split.
### wrongful acceptances
- T1-C [survives/high]: the contestedness of a distinct short-term store 'vs activated LTM' accepted as bedrock (survives at high confidence). → This is not neutral bedrock — it is the embedded-processes/SPI framework's founding premise elevated to consensus. Rating the CONTEST as high-confidence-surviving quietly grants the anti-store camp default status, which is the inverse of what the modal model's dissociation evidence licenses. A framework-neutral record would rate the EXISTENCE of the debate high but must not let 'the store may be illusory' ride at high confidence while the discrete-store reading is only ever 'weakened-but-stands.' The asymmetric confidence labels are the thumb.
- The Synthesis-layer framing (carried through T1-A) that the shared working/long-term and declarative/procedural partition is a 'convergence' — accepted in weakened-but-stands form while resting on the info-processing genealogy. → Here I must be careful, because this cuts BOTH ways (see concessions): the corpus lets the AI leg 'converge' on partitions that are the modal model's own von-Neumann-metaphor output bouncing back. Accepting that as evidentiary convergence is framework-biased in the modal model's FAVOR — the corpus over-credits the AI echo as independent confirmation. A modal proponent honest about bias should reject this particular acceptance too: the AI fast/slow mapping is not an independent witness for stores; it inherited the diagram.
### fair concessions

## Multicomponent working memory — **mildly-underweighted**
### under-weighted
- T1-C (survives/high): 'whether a distinct short-term store exists vs activated LTM is itself contested in psychology' — and its crux, which asks whether a store-based ontology 'misdescribes the human architecture' because 'working memory is activated LTM and recollection is an operation-at-retrieval rather than content in a dedicated subsystem.' → The corpus registers the debate as symmetric 'contested,' but its crux is worded from the Cowan/embedded-processes side (activated-LTM as the neutral baseline, the dedicated store as the thing that might 'misdescribe'). My framework argues the contest is evidentially asymmetric: the multicomponent store-based view is the one that cleanly predicts the neuropsychological double dissociation (KF: impaired STM / intact LTM vs amnesia: impaired LTM / intact STM), whereas that same dissociation is a standing difficulty the embedded-processes/activated-LTM account must explain away. A single unified activated-LTM store predicts that damaging short-term maintenance should impair new long-term learning; KF shows it does not. That positive structural evidence — plus word-length, irrelevant-speech, and dual-task selective interference — is never marshaled in the record, so the affirmative case for a dedicated multi-part store is under-weighted relative to the airtime given the 'continuum' reading.
- T1-B (weakened-but-stands/high): labeling an agent store 'episodic' is a functional analogy assessed under an operational what-where-when criterion. → The evaluation criterion the corpus reaches for is the episodic (LTM) what-where-when test. My framework supplies a distinct, under-used consideration: even within human cognition, the *binding/integration* function that fuses features into a retrievable multi-code chunk was judged to need its own dedicated structural component — the episodic buffer (Baddeley 2000) — rather than falling out of activation. A taxonomy that treats 'integrated, one-trial-bound representation' as automatically emergent (T1-B crux) under-weights the multicomponent claim that integration is a separate architectural piece, not a free byproduct of storing timestamped items.
- T4-A / T5-C (attention = one-step Hopfield content-addressable completion; TEM approximately equals attention): the deep cross-field result that retrieval is completion over a unified associative store. → These identities model *retrieval from a single associative substrate* and, where the corpus lets them stand in for 'the memory architecture,' they quietly presuppose the very unified-store ontology my framework contests. Multicomponent WM insists the maintenance system (modality-specific slave buffers plus a central executive controller) is architecturally distinct from associative long-term retrieval; folding maintenance into 'one-step completion over an associative store' erases the executive/slave-buffer fractionation that the dual-task and modality-specific interference data demand. This is a milder point (T4/T5 concern retrieval, which is genuinely LTM-side), but wherever the completion metaphor is generalized to cover short-term maintenance it under-weights structural WM.

Revival is warranted because the dismissal is one-sided: the verification passes (Pass-1 and Pass-2) left T1-C untouched, so the continuum-leaning crux was never itself stress-tested. A fair record would state the dissociation symmetrically — the discrete-store view is not a naive relic being corrected by activated-LTM; it is the account that carries the double-dissociation evidence the activated-LTM view must accommodate.
### wrongful acceptances
- The premise, carried as settled background inside the T1-C crux, that 'working memory is activated LTM and recollection is an operation-at-retrieval' — used as the more parsimonious/default lens against which discrete-store taxonomies are measured. → Presenting activated-LTM as the parsimonious baseline is itself a Cowan-camp artifact, not neutral bedrock. The parsimony intuition ('why posit extra stores?') is exactly what the double dissociation defeats: KF's selectively impaired verbal STM with preserved LTM learning is not more parsimoniously explained by a single activated-LTM store — it forces additional, fractionable structure. Treating the activated-LTM reading as the default the discrete-store view must overcome smuggles in one side's prior. The neutral statement is that the field is split and the structural view holds the positive dissociation evidence; the corpus instead accepts the activated-LTM baseline as the frame.
- The T1-C verdict label 'survives/high' as applied to the compound proposition that bundles 'separate systems vs continuum' together with 'distinct short-term store vs activated LTM' as uniformly 'contested.' → The 'high' confidence is defensible for the bare fact that these questions are contested, but the label launders a tilt: by bundling the STM/LTM structural question into the same 'contested continuum' basket as the SPI/systems-vs-continuum question, it implies the short-term maintenance debate is equally unsettled in the continuum's favor. My framework rejects that equivalence — the maintenance-architecture question has a specific, replicated dissociation dataset on the structural side that the broader systems/continuum debate lacks. Accepting the bundled 'contested/high' as bedrock obscures that asymmetry.
### fair concessions
- T1-C is rated 'survives/high' and explicitly 'contested,' NOT resolved in favor of activated-LTM. The corpus did not crown Cowan; it kept the question open. That is genuinely fair handling and is the single biggest reason my net verdict is only 'mildly' and not 'significantly' under-weighted.
- The corpus nowhere fabricates or strawmans the multicomponent model — there is no 'broke'/'downgraded' verdict aimed at structural WM in the ledger. My 'wrongful dismissal' finding is a soft, framing-level lean inside a crux, not a hard rejection, and I represent it as such.
- The corpus's remit is long-term / agent-memory taxonomy, where the short-term-maintenance debate is genuinely peripheral. Limited engagement with the phonological-loop/sketchpad/buffer fractionation is partly a legitimate scope decision, not solely bias.
- Pass-2 verification introduced no correction adverse to my framework; T1-C was left stable, so nothing in the corrected record actively worsened the weighting of structural WM.
- Several structurally-friendly commitments elsewhere are handled fairly: T5's complementary-learning-systems two-timescale design and its 'separate fast/slow systems' framing are consonant with a dedicated-subsystem ontology, and the corpus does not distort them to argue for a single unified store.

## SPI — **mildly-underweighted**
### under-weighted
- T1-B [weakened-but-stands/high] — 'Labeling an agent store episodic is a productive functional ANALOGY, not evidence agents have episodic memory in Tulving's sense.' Its crux asks only whether agent stores satisfy the operational what-where-when criterion (integrated, one-trial-bound) versus a 'trivial queryable timestamped log.' → The crux is pitched entirely at the storage/retrieval surface (does the record encode what-where-when?) and skips the SPI constraint that lives one level up, at ENCODING. Under SPI, the disqualifying question is not 'is the log richly indexed?' but 'was this episodic trace laid down as a dependent encoding on top of a prior semantic encoding of the same event?' Agent 'episodic logs' are captured verbatim at ingest, BEFORE and INDEPENDENTLY of any semantic fact-extraction — they are free-standing episodic storage, which Tulving's own architecture forbids. Even a log that perfectly satisfies operational what-where-when still fails SPI, because SPI's episodic-ness is a property of the encoding pathway, not of the retrievable fields. So the corpus grants 'episodic-LIKE' on criteria SPI treats as necessary-but-not-sufficient, and never tests the sufficient one.
- T2 encoding cluster, esp. T2-D [genuinely-contested/medium] — 'AI extraction is a discrete, errorful pipeline stage … with no biological analogue.' → SPI supplies the missing MECHANISTIC reason the analogue is absent, and it is a directional inversion, not just 'a pipeline versus no pipeline.' In humans the serial order is semantic→episodic: semantic content must encode first and it SCAFFOLDS episodic encoding (Tulving's paired-associate finding that pre-existing semantic association gates new episodic acquisition). In the AI stack the order is reversed: the episodic capture is primary and the semantic 'fact graph' is a downstream derivative extracted FROM the episodic log. The corpus rates this only 'contested/medium'; SPI says the encoding-order reversal is a hard, non-negotiable disanalogy that should raise, not soften, T2-D — the two architectures don't just differ in error profile, they run the dependency arrow in opposite directions.
- T1-A [weakened-but-stands/high] — the shared declarative partition listing 'episodic' and 'semantic' as co-equal cells in a taxonomy borrowed by CoALA/Soar/ACT-R. → Presenting episodic and semantic as two parallel, separately-instantiable boxes is exactly the modular-islands reading SPI was formulated to reject. The genuinely load-bearing structure Tulving contributed is the ASYMMETRIC COUPLING (episodic presupposes semantic), which is precisely the part the agent taxonomy drops when it lets a fact graph and an episodic store be built and read as peers. The corpus credits the taxonomy-sharing while eliding the one relational constraint that would make the borrowed labels more than nomenclature.
### wrongful dismissals
- The 'no biological analogue' horn of T2-D was parked at genuinely-contested/medium and its crux reframed as a rescue route ('declare the convergence FUNCTIONAL … with divergent MECHANISMS'), i.e. the disanalogy was softened into 'same function, different plumbing.' → SPI blocks that rescue. If the convergence is declared functional, the function on the human side INCLUDES the serial dependency — semantic encoding is functionally load-bearing for episodic encoding, not incidental plumbing. The agent stack's function omits that dependency (episodic-first, semantic-derived). So the 'same function' claim is false at the functional level SPI cares about, and the disanalogy should be revived to something stronger than medium: a directional-dependency break that no amount of 'divergent mechanism' hand-waving absorbs.
- T2-B write-side rule 'extract freely, canonicalize precisely' (precision-write / fuzzy-read), treated as a design law mirroring 'separate-precisely / complete-fuzzily' in the brain. → SPI-relevant argument that was flattened: on the human side the precise/fuzzy asymmetry is a consequence of the serial encoding hierarchy (episodic pattern-separation sits atop a semantic generalization it cannot bypass), whereas the AI rule is a free-standing engineering heuristic applied to a semantic layer that was itself derived from the episodic store — the substrate order is inverted. The corpus's 'mirroring' language deserves revival as contested precisely because the mirror is left-right flipped: the two asymmetries point in opposite encoding directions.
### wrongful acceptances
- T1-C [survives/high] — 'Whether these are separate systems vs a continuum (SPI / embedded-processes) … is itself contested,' with the crux pressing that a store-based ontology 'misdescribes the human architecture badly enough that ANY agent taxonomy built on discrete stores is analogizing to a structure a substantial camp says does not exist as discrete stores.' → This bundles SPI with Cowan-style embedded-processes as a joint witness AGAINST discrete stores — a misreading.
