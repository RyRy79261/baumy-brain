## Replication Verdict

**1) Converge or diverge — and toward what.**
They **diverged**, unanimously. All 5 self-labeled convergence with the incumbent as only *"partial"*; none endorsed "plural symbolic stores + weighted knowledge-graph" as the frame. They diverged toward one shared alternative, statable in a sentence they all effectively wrote: **a process-first, relationship-indexed, two-speed reconstructive memory** — fast episodic capture → slow consolidation into person/relationship gist → **retrieval as generative reconstruction, not lookup**. The decisive move, made independently by all five, is to **demote "stores" to derived artifacts and promote PROCESSES (consolidation + reconstruction) to load-bearing.** Re-deriver 3 says it outright ("the load-bearing element is NOT a taxonomy of stores — it is the pair of PROCESSES the stores fall out of"); re-deriver 5 concedes the incumbent's structure survives only as "a DERIVED consolidation artifact, not the capture format." So the incumbent isn't refuted — it's **relocated**: our weighted knowledge-graph is exactly what 4/5 independently reconstruct as the *slow consolidated layer*, but never as the organizing frame.

**2) What multiple re-derivers landed on that the incumbent under-weights or omits.**
Ranked by independence of agreement:

- **Reconstructive retrieval / gist-over-verbatim (5/5).** Recall = *regenerate* from gist + cues + current goal, hedged with confidence ("I think you mentioned…"), not return matched rows. Our retrieve.ts returns ranked evidence/facts; there is no gist-distillation or reconstruction step. This is the single biggest omission, and it's the one they each tie *directly* to the "human, not robotic" objective.
- **Two-speed CLS consolidation with anchor episodes (5/5).** Our `reflect` cron is a partial match, but it distills *facts/profiles via trust-supersession*, not *gist with a few retained vivid episodes*. The re-derivers want episodes preserved as texture/evidence while regularities semanticize — we flatten to triples.
- **Common-ground / transactive ledger (4/5).** "What I already told you," "what's mutually acknowledged," "who-knows/forgets-what" as first-class memory objects (Clark & Brennan; Wegner; Hirst & Echterhoff). **The incumbent has nothing here** — a clean omission, and one specific to *conversation* rather than memory-in-general.
- **Need-probability ranking + rational forgetting (5/5, Anderson & Schooler 1991).** Rank by *predicted future conversational need* (recency × frequency × spacing × salience); deliberate decay is a feature. We rank by RRF similarity + recency composition and never deliberately forget for relevance.
- **Episodes as the atomic unit, tagged with affect + temporal-context (5/5).** Our atomic unit is the fact/triple (+ evidence item); "who was there / how it felt / that night we argued" is discarded into edge metadata. Re-derivers 1–2 argue this collapse is *the* thing that makes an agent "sound like a lookup table."
- **Reconsolidation / write-on-recall (3/5).** Every retrieval re-labilizes and updates the trace — remembering is an update op. We treat retrieval as read-only.
- **Confidence/provenance calibration surfaced in output (4/5).** We store trust/provenance but never hedge the reply by trace strength.

Convergence *with* the incumbent (worth noting for fairness): **bitemporal supersession** (3/5 name it — we already have it) and **provenance/trust-gating** (we arguably do this *better* than they specify; our poisoning defense is more concrete than their "transactive stance").

**3) The recurring organizing structure for the conversational objective specifically.**
**Person-and-relationship as the top-level index — not topics, not facts, not time — 5/5.** Every re-deriver re-cast Conway & Pleydell-Pearce's Self-Memory System with the *relationship* (dyad/group) swapped in as the organizing axis, and each argued naturalness *falls out of* recalling "us" rather than recalling rows. The full recurring spine: **relationship-indexed → two-speed (episodic ledger + consolidated person-models) → reconstruction readout → common-ground ledger threaded through.** Our incumbent is fact/entity-indexed with persons as merely frequent nodes; the reserved-but-unused `entities.name_embedding` and the per-person `reflect` profile are the only gestures toward person-primacy, and both are peripheral, not the frame.

**4) Would we get the same result on replay? — the honest answer.**
**Yes, robustly — with a real caveat.** Five lenses seeded differently (function-first memory science, molecular/systems neuro, CLS engineering, social-grounding, temporal-context) converged on the *same* frame and cite an overlapping canon: **McClelland/McNaughton/O'Reilly 1995 (CLS) in 5/5; Anderson & Schooler 1991 in 5/5; Conway & Pleydell-Pearce 2000 in 4/5; Bartlett reconstruction in 4/5; Clark & Brennan grounding in 4/5; Nader reconsolidation in 3/5.** That is high inter-derivation agreement, not noise; a replay would again produce process-first, relationship-indexed, reconstructive, two-speed — and would again re-derive our knowledge-graph only as the *consolidated layer*.

The caveat that keeps this honest: **the convergence is partly structurally induced, not fully independent.** (a) All five drew from the same cognitive/neuroscience-of-memory literature pool, so shared canon guarantees some shared conclusion. (b) The objective prompt itself contains "recalls naturally rather than robotic database lookups" — that phrasing *primes* reconstruction-over-lookup, so the re-derivers may be reflecting the prompt back at us. (c) **None of the five took a systems-engineering / IR / production-retrieval lens** — the incumbent's home territory. A sixth re-deriver reasoning from latency, precision, auditability, and the security invariants (injection wall, trust-gated writes, exactly-once) would likely have landed much closer to the incumbent, because reconstruction-at-recall is in direct tension with our golden rule ("deterministic code disposes") and our poisoning defenses. So the 5/5 is best read as: **from the cognitive-plausibility direction the result is highly reproducible and the incumbent is genuinely under-weighting process; but the sample is blind to the engineering/safety axis on which the incumbent was actually chosen.** The strongest replayable finding isn't "replace the graph" — it's "the graph is right but mis-framed: it's the slow layer, and we are missing the fast episodic layer, the reconstruction read step, and the common-ground ledger on top of it."
