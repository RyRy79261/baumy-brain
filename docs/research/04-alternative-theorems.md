# ALTERNATIVE-THEOREM SYNTHESIS

*Objective held fixed throughout: a human-like conversational companion that remembers people, relationships, shared history, and prior conversations, and recalls them **naturally rather than as robotic database lookups**. The incumbent under evaluation is Baumy's "plural symbolic stores + weighted knowledge-graph" — fact/entity-indexed bitemporal triples, hybrid-RRF retrieval, a 6-hour `reflect` profile cron, trust-gated supersession, encrypted secrets, exactly-once reminders.*

The headline result is a **double convergence**. Two independent processes — (a) five blind re-derivations seeded from different literatures, and (b) ten adversarially-built competing theorems scored by Opus — landed on the *same* organizing frame. That agreement across methods is the strongest evidence in the whole exercise, and it is also where the sharpest honesty is required, because both processes share a blind spot.

---

## I. Replication verdict — would we land here again?

**They diverged from the incumbent, unanimously, and converged on one alternative.** All five re-derivers self-labeled convergence with "plural stores + weighted graph" as only *partial*; none endorsed it as the organizing frame. They independently reconstructed the same alternative, statable in one sentence: **a process-first, relationship-indexed, two-speed reconstructive memory** — fast episodic capture → slow consolidation into person/relationship gist → **retrieval as generative reconstruction, not lookup**. The decisive shared move is to **demote "stores" to derived artifacts and promote the PROCESSES (consolidation + reconstruction) to load-bearing.**

Crucially, the incumbent was **not refuted — it was relocated.** 4/5 re-derivers reconstruct our weighted knowledge-graph exactly, but as the *slow consolidated layer*, never as the top-level frame.

**What multiple re-derivers landed on that the incumbent under-weights or omits** (ranked by independence of agreement):

| Finding | Independence | Incumbent status |
|---|---|---|
| Reconstructive retrieval / gist-over-verbatim (recall = regenerate from gist + cue + goal, hedged) | 5/5 | **Absent** — `retrieve.ts` returns ranked rows; no gist-distillation or reconstruction step. The single biggest omission, and the one each ties directly to "human, not robotic." |
| Two-speed CLS consolidation with retained vivid anchor episodes | 5/5 | Partial — `reflect` distills *profiles via trust-supersession*, not *gist + anchor episodes*. |
| Common-ground / transactive ledger ("what I already told you," who-knows-what) | 4/5 | **Clean omission** — and the one specific to *conversation* rather than memory-in-general. |
| Need-probability ranking + rational forgetting (Anderson & Schooler 1991) | 5/5 | Absent — RRF similarity + recency; never ranks by predicted future need, never deliberately forgets for relevance. |
| Episodes as atomic unit, tagged with affect + temporal context ("who was there / how it felt") | 5/5 | Absent — atomic unit is the triple; episode texture is discarded into edge metadata. |
| Reconsolidation / write-on-recall | 3/5 | Absent — retrieval is read-only. |
| Confidence/provenance surfaced in the reply | 4/5 | Stored but never voiced as hedge. |

**Convergence *with* the incumbent** (for fairness): bitemporal supersession (3/5 name it — we have it) and provenance/trust-gating (we arguably do this *better* than they specify).

**The recurring organizing structure, specifically for conversation:** **person-and-relationship as the top-level index** — not topics, facts, or time — **5/5**. Every re-deriver re-cast Conway & Pleydell-Pearce's Self-Memory System (2000) with the *relationship* swapped in as the axis, arguing naturalness *falls out of* recalling "us" rather than rows. Our incumbent is fact/entity-indexed; the reserved `entities.name_embedding` and the per-person `reflect` profile are the only gestures toward person-primacy, and both are peripheral.

**Would we replay this? Yes, robustly — with one honest caveat.** Five differently-seeded lenses converged and cite an overlapping canon: McClelland/McNaughton/O'Reilly 1995 (CLS) 5/5; Anderson & Schooler 1991 5/5; Conway & Pleydell-Pearce 2000 4/5; Bartlett 1932 4/5; Clark & Brennan 1991 4/5; Nader et al. 2000 3/5. That is high inter-derivation agreement, not noise.

The caveat that keeps it honest — and it is the same blind spot the theorem-scoring shares: **the convergence is partly structurally induced.** (a) All drew from the same cognitive/neuroscience-of-memory pool. (b) The objective prompt *itself* says "recalls naturally rather than robotic database lookups," which **primes** reconstruction-over-lookup — the re-derivers may be reflecting the prompt back. (c) **None reasoned from a systems-engineering / IR / production-retrieval / security lens — the incumbent's home turf.** A sixth re-deriver reasoning from latency, precision, auditability, and Baumy's own invariants (injection wall, trust-gated writes, exactly-once) would have landed much closer to the incumbent, because reconstruction-at-recall is in *direct tension* with "deterministic code disposes" and the memory-poisoning defense. So the correct reading: **from the cognitive-plausibility direction the result is highly reproducible and the incumbent genuinely under-weights process; but the sample is blind to the axis on which the incumbent was actually chosen.** The strongest replayable finding is not "replace the graph" — it is **"the graph is right but mis-framed: it is the slow layer, and we are missing the fast episodic layer, the reconstruction read step, and the common-ground ledger on top of it."**

## II. Frameworks & data we missed that matter (esp. for a conversational companion)

The prior passes were dominated by lab-cognitive and computational-modeling traditions. The re-hunt surfaces six clusters that are *orthogonal* to those and disproportionately on-target for a companion. I rank them by conversational relevance, not generality.

**Cluster A — The common-ground / transactive ledger (the biggest clean omission, and it is conversation-specific).**
This is the schema the incumbent lacks entirely. Clark & Brennan (1991) grounding + Clark (1996) *personal vs. communal* common ground + Clark & Marshall (1981) "reference diaries": a companion needs a **per-dyad ledger of what was jointly established**, distinct from what it knows *about* a person. Horton & Gerrig (2005, 2016) sharpen it into a *mechanism* — common ground is not a consulted database but an emergent property of ordinary memory, with **partner-identity as the retrieval cue** (person-as-cue resurfacing, not profile-lookup) — which is a competing organizing theorem in itself, directly opposing the RAG idiom. Wegner (1985/1987) transactive memory adds the **who-knows-what directory**; Heritage (2012) / Kamio (1997) / Labov & Fanshel's A-events/B-events add **epistemic rights** (whose story it is to tell, so the bot doesn't leak A's confidence to B or tell people their own business); Gopie & MacLeod (2009) *destination memory* adds the **outbound anti-repetition ledger** (memory for *to-whom-I-told-what* is worse than source memory → story repetition; a companion that quietly tracks this is superhumanly considerate). *For a shared-house group bot, this cluster is the most apt missing piece of all.*

**Cluster B — Deployed companion-memory engineering (the axis the re-derivers were blind to; this is where the incumbent gets validated).**
The prior passes never engaged the applied lineage that *is* today's companion-memory backend. Bickmore & Picard (2005, *Relational Agents*, TOCHI) is the single most direct HCI blueprint — memory as relationship-maintenance *behaviors* (continuity behaviors, reference to shared past, meta-relational talk), validated in a 101-user month-long RCT. Bae et al. (2022, EMNLP, NAVER CareCall, "Keep Me Updated!") independently re-derived **APPEND/REPLACE/DELETE supersession** from a real deployment (outdated memories actively confuse later chat) — direct field validation of our bitemporal design. MemoryBank/SiliconFriend (Zhong et al., AAAI 2024) transplants Ebbinghaus decay + an *evolving user portrait*; Generative Agents (Park et al., UIST 2023) uses recency×importance×relevance + a *reflection* loop (episodes → inferred traits) that is precisely our `reflect`; MemGPT (Packer et al. 2023) / Mem0 / A-MEM / Zep-Graphiti define the production idiom. Judy Kay's **scrutable user models** / Personis (a pre-LLM literature on persistent, *user-inspectable/correctable* person-models) partly answers the auditability worry the cognitive frames ignore. And the benchmarks — **LoCoMo** (Maharana et al., ACL 2024), **LongMemEval** (Wu et al., ICLR 2025) — pin the measurable failure surface (temporal reasoning, knowledge *updates*, and **abstention** — knowing when you don't remember) *while explicitly not scoring conversational naturalness*. Takeaway: the incumbent is a defensible member of this family; the frontier beyond it is agent-curated self-editing memory and dual-persona consistency.

**Cluster C — What to keep, and how to phrase it (the naturalness surface is partly a *surface-realization* problem).**
Stafford & Daly (1984) / Brown-Schmidt & Benjamin (2018): people recall **~10% of idea units** after 5 minutes; surface form is lost almost immediately and replaced by gist, biased to self/relationally-salient moments. So *verbatim total recall is behaviorally non-human and reads as surveillance*. Cox & Ooi (2023) show experimentally that **paraphrased/gist references land in the sweet spot** while verbatim playback maximizes both social presence *and* creepiness (the personalization-privacy paradox). Sellen & Whittaker (2010, CACM, "Beyond Total Capture") gives the five Rs (Recollecting / Reminiscing / Retrieving / Reflecting / Remembering-intentions) and the "**cue, don't replay**" finding. Grosz & Sidner (1986) / Gundel-Hedberg-Zacharski (1993) givenness hierarchy / Ariel accessibility: referring-expression form ("that plumber saga" vs. "on 2026-03-14 you reported a leak") *is* a mapping from assumed memory status — **robotic lookups are literally givenness-hierarchy violations.** Reeves & Nass (1996) / Nass & Moon (2000) CASA: being remembered signals caring, being forgotten signals disregard, remembering-too-much violates norms — the expectation baseline.

**Cluster D — Memory is *built in dialogue* (the "it learns" mechanism, conversation-specific).**
Nelson & Fivush (2004): autobiographical memory *emerges through* elaborative joint reminiscing, not solo maturation — the most on-objective developmental result. A companion that reminisces elaboratively doesn't just retrieve, it **co-forms** the shared record. Bluck & Alea (TALE) functions-of-autobiographical-memory (self/social/directive) gives a *why-recall* organizing policy (bonding/continuity/direction over relevance-only ranking). Hirst & Echterhoff (2012) social-shared retrieval-induced forgetting: *what the bot chooses to retell becomes the canonical joint narrative and what it omits fades* — retelling is a consolidation *policy*, and an ethical one. Einstein & McDaniel prospective memory reframes reminders as the *same faculty* as recall (mental time travel), not a separate cron.

**Cluster E — The organizing index and the two-layer person model.**
Conway & Pleydell-Pearce SMS (relationship-swapped) as the spine; Hastie & Kumar (1980) / Klein & Loftus (1993) **trait-summary ⟂ episode dissociation** (distilled person-summaries queryable *without* the episodes that built them, plus preferential retention of expectancy-*incongruent* behavior — how we actually know friends); Nelson & Gruendel **scripts** (compress recurring interactions, remember the surprising deviation); Dunbar's layered social network (5/15/50/150 → allocate *memory resolution by relationship closeness*, not uniformly); Qi Wang (2016) cultural-dynamic theory (no culture-neutral "right" granularity — adapt self-vs-relational framing and emotional emphasis to the user's style).

**Cluster F — The ethical/philosophical charter + boundary data.**
Clark & Chalmers (1998) extended mind + Sutton (2010) **complementarity** (an external memory should *not* wholesale mimic human forgetting — pair archival stability with reconstructive presentation); Heersmink (2017–2022) distributed-narrative-self / evocative objects (the single most on-point recent philosophy — a companion is a constitutive part of the user's self-narrative, with measurable integration dimensions); Margalit (2002) / Campbell (2006) ethics of memory (thick relations carry *duties* to remember and to forget-on-request; retellings can honor or gaslight; *faithfulness to the shared past*, not retrieval precision, is the success criterion); the Replika grief/harm studies (Laestadius et al. 2022; Skjuve et al.; Pentina et al.) — **memory discontinuity is experienced as bereavement, not a QA bug**; Leite et al. (2013) / Kanda et al. (2004) long-term HRI — visible interpersonal memory is *the* variable separating sustained companionship from abandonment, and ill-calibrated memory reads as creepy; and the security face the cognitive frames never contemplate: **Chen et al. (ACNS 2023, "Those Aren't Your Memories, They're Somebody Else's")** — adversarial writes into long-term chatbot memory, which *validates our trust-gating as necessary, not paranoid.* Boundary data: HSAM (perfect recall is neither accurate nor socially desirable) and SDAM (people run functioning relationships on gist/trait knowledge with *no* episodic re-experiencing) together show the semantic person-model, not the event log, carries the relational weight.

## III. Strongest competing theorems — ranked by fitToObjective, then fitToData

**First, the meta-finding that mirrors §I:** the ten theorems collapse into **three families that converge on one core** — *recall = reconstruction conditioned on the present, over a relationship-indexed, two-speed substrate.* This convergence, arrived at adversarially and independently of the blind re-derivations, is why the frame in §IV is robust.

- **Reconstruction family** (memory = generation, not readout): predictive-generative, rate-distortion, reconstructive-goal, instance-primary, dynamical-attractor, narrative-self. Several invoke the **Ramsauer et al. (2020)** proof that modern-Hopfield associative memory *is* transformer attention, to argue the LLM substrate is *already* reconstructive — so lookup is the foreign graft that produces the "robotic" seam.
- **Social/communicative family** (memory = relationship-maintaining, addressee-indexed): social-transactive, communicative-relevance.
- **Access/interface family**: workspace-attention (adds the metamemory self-model).

All ten scored "better" on fitToObjective; eight scored "better" on fitToData, two "comparable." Per the sort, the eight (better/better) rank above the two (better/comparable); within ties I order by verdict strength × conversational-centrality.

**Tier 1 (better / better):**

**1. Social-relational / transactive** — *Opus: "strongest objective-fit of any competitor."* **Genuinely adds:** the unit of memory is the *dyad/group + its transactive links*, not the item in one head; **per-dyad common ground**; and its highest-leverage, *lowest-risk* export — the **who-knows-what directory promoted to a volunteered output** ("I don't hold that — Marta does / it's on the fridge / you told me Tuesday"). Uniquely apt for Baumy's *actual* setting (a group house), and *safe*: it operates on pointers and provenance, not on reconstructing security-critical content, so it delivers the benefit without crossing the poisoning boundary. Caveat: mechanistically thin, weak cold-start; its central operation ("defer to the shared account") *is* poisoning if applied to the fidelity core — hence complement, not replacement.

**2. Rate-distortion / predictive-compression** — *Opus: "the strongest of the competing memory theorems for a conversational companion."* **Genuinely adds:** the **distortion metric as the load-bearing design choice** (which errors are costly *in this relationship* — a friend's dog's name yes, which Tuesday a remark was made no), replacing a scalar importance×recency; **surprise-gated retention** (keep the high-prediction-error residual, fold the predictable); **forgetting = re-compression on a need-probability schedule**; the **per-person/dyad generative model + shared codebook** as the central data structures; and the **dual-code prescription** (gist reconstruction constrained by a verbatim anchor) that resolves the naturalness/fidelity tension. Makes the human/LLM correspondence a *mathematical identity* (both are lossy predictive codecs). Rigorous empirical spine (Sims 2018; Nagy/Török/Orbán 2020; Gershman).

**3. Predictive-processing / generative reconstruction** — *Opus: "the strongest reorganizing theorem yet fielded."* **Genuinely adds:** the **substrate-identity coup** — on an LLM, memory and language are the *same* generative object, so in-voice, gist-first, context-conditioned recall is the **default by construction**, dissolving the retrieve-rows-then-re-narrate seam; the unification of **memory + imagination + planning** with a *confirmed* cross-domain prediction the store frame cannot make (hippocampal amnesics also fail to *imagine* futures — Hassabis et al. 2007, PNAS); reframing the **`reflect` cron as generative replay = the actual learning engine** (how a companion learns a person without catastrophically forgetting old relationships — van de Ven et al. 2020); and updating as **precision-weighted reconsolidation** (predicts *when* a correction sticks — no-surprise corrections shouldn't churn the model), reinterpreting our trust-gating as precision weighting.

**4. Reconstructive / goal-serving (Bartlett-primary)** — **Genuinely adds:** the cleanest statement of the *inversion* — recall = `argmax_r U(r | constraints, cue, present goal)`, where **accuracy is one term in utility, not the criterion**, and the canonical distortions (Bartlett leveling/sharpening/assimilation, DRM, misinformation) are *the fingerprint of the system computing its real objective correctly.* Its practical payoff is the **theoretical grounding for "LLM proposes, deterministic code disposes" + an encrypted verbatim vault**: hallucination *is* human reconstructive false memory, and the remedy is a fuzzy-trace verbatim gate, not "retrieve more."

**5. Narrative / autobiographical-self (Conway SMS)** — **Genuinely adds:** the **per-relationship *narrative* as the top-level spine**, and the ability to **narrate change** ("you used to hate cilantro, now you love it") rather than silently superseding a row — the arc *is* what makes recall feel like a relationship. Predicts the *direction* of distortion (toward present-self coherence + gist), the reminiscence bump, and the self-reference effect, which a neutral supersession/decay rule cannot. **Coherence-vs-correspondence** is the principled "human vs. archival" knob.

**6. Global-workspace / attention-centric** — **Genuinely adds:** a first-class **metamemory / attention-schema layer** (feeling-of-knowing, "that reminds me…", "I might be mixing this up," "I don't think you've told me that yet") and the **storage-strength ⟂ retrieval-strength dissociation** (Bjork & Bjork 1992) that represents the deeply human state *"solidly known but not accessible right now"* (tip-of-the-tongue, resurfacing when talk returns to the right context) — states a single activation value *cannot* encode. This is the conversational connective tissue; metamemory *leads* content rather than lagging it.

**7. Instance / global-matching (MINERVA/REM)** — **Genuinely adds:** **relocating abstraction from write-time to read-time** — store context-preserving raw *exemplars* (who/when/mood/thread/source) and compute "who is X," gist, and profiles *on demand* as a similarity-weighted echo. This turns our frozen, destructively-superseding `reflect`-fact into an **always-current, query-dependent, non-destructive view**, and holds contradiction as *graded, recoverable belief*. Preserves the phrasing/source/context that make recall sound human.

**8. Dynamical-systems / attractor-trajectory** — **Genuinely adds:** **people-as-basins** (native coreference, partial-cue completion, "you know who I mean" as pattern completion — which our `resolveEntity` already approximates discretely); **representational drift** (re-embed a person under a new model without losing the memory, provided the manifold is preserved); and the most useful **diagnostic CLS split** — it names *which* parts should *settle* (relational recall) vs. *fetch* (records), converting "make it less robotic" into a concrete boundary.

**Tier 2 (better fitToObjective / comparable fitToData — demoted only by the fitToData tiebreak; on pure objective-centrality both rank near the top):**

**9. Communicative / relevance-theoretic** — the *deepest* theory of *why* conversational recall is human: memory is **prospective and addressee-indexed at encoding** (you retain based on who you'll tell), recall is **assembled at the point of telling** for optimal relevance to *this* partner's common ground, and **Gricean suppression of already-shared ground** is a direct naturalness win a coverage-maximizer fights. Its **trust-weighted common-ground repair** is a principled, first-class poisoning defense. Demoted to 9 *only* because its fitToData is comparable — it strains on non-communicative memory (procedural/perceptual) and on pre-linguistic episodic memory (scrub jays; rat replay), which have no audience.

**10. Enactive / ecological / anti-representational** — a genuinely alternative *ontology* (remembering = skilled re-attunement of a brain-body-world system) whose real cash value is a **routing thesis** and one sharp, testable prediction: the **"verbatim uncanny valley"** — accurate *gist* re-tellings rated warmer/more human than accurate *verbatim* quotes, with **adaptive forgetting toward gist as a feature, not a failure.** Best used as a design corrective/evaluation lens, not a substrate.

## IV. Is there a better organizing frame for human-like conversational memory? Yes — and its shape.

**Yes.** Both the blind re-derivations (§I) and the theorem families (§III) converge on it, which is why it should be taken seriously despite the shared blind spot. Call it **relationship-indexed, two-speed reconstructive memory over a firewalled fidelity ledger** (the re-hunt independently named the missed cluster "relational-reconstructive memory").

It is *better than the incumbent for the stated objective* not because the graph is wrong, but because it **inverts primacy**: the incumbent makes the *store* primary and bolts fluency on top (producing exactly the retrieve-rows-then-re-narrate seam the objective names); the better frame makes **reconstruction + relationship primary** and wraps a small addressable verbatim/audit store around it. Its shape (descriptive — *not* a design):

1. **Top-level index = person/relationship**, not fact/topic/time (Conway SMS relationship-swapped; 5/5 re-derivers; narrative-self, social-transactive). Naturalness falls out of recalling "us."

2. **Two speeds (CLS).** A **fast episodic ledger** — raw, context-rich, affect-tagged, who-was-there, temporally-ordered (instance-primary + Clayton & Dickinson's what-where-when criterion) — feeding **slow consolidation** into per-person/dyad **gist + a few retained vivid anchor episodes** (McClelland/McNaughton/O'Reilly 1995; rate-distortion; predictive-generative's replay). This is the "it learns" engine.

3. **Recall = reconstruction, not lookup:** regenerate a gist-first account conditioned on cue + present context + goal + addressee, in-voice, hedged (the 5/5 biggest omission; the reconstruction family's shared core). This is the direct cure for "robotic database lookup."

4. **A common-ground / transactive ledger threaded through:** per-dyad "what we've established," a who-knows-what directory, an outbound anti-repetition ledger, epistemic-rights tracking (Clark & Brennan; Wegner; Horton & Gerrig; Heritage; Gopie & MacLeod). Conversation-*specific*; the incumbent has nothing here; **for a group house bot it is the most apt addition** and (per social-transactive) the *safest*, since it traffics in pointers/provenance, not reconstructed content.

5. **Need-probability ranking + adaptive forgetting** (Anderson & Schooler 1991; Frankland & Josselyn; rate-distortion; 5/5): rank by predicted future *conversational* need; deliberately decay low-value detail to gist. Forgetting is curation — and total recall reads as surveillance (Stafford & Daly; Cox & Ooi; Sellen & Whittaker).

6. **A metamemory surface** (workspace-attention; 4/5): hedging, feeling-of-knowing, "known-but-not-accessible-now," "you haven't told me that." The connective tissue that reads as human.

7. **Memory built in dialogue** (Nelson & Fivush; Hirst & Echterhoff): elaborative co-narration as an *encoding* mechanism, not silent server-side extraction; prospective follow-ups as the same faculty as recall.

8. **The incumbent, relocated and firewalled.** The symbolic store + trust-gated bitemporal triples + encrypted secrets + exactly-once reminders + audit are **retained** — *not* as the frame but as (a) the slow consolidated layer and (b) the **verbatim/audit "life-raft"** for the fidelity-critical, security-load-bearing, deletable tail (door codes, wifi, rent dates, promises, grants, deletion). Every theorem that wins the objective *also concedes this*: reconstruction is structurally unsafe here (confabulation, unprovable deletion, poisoning-by-blending). The reconstructive-goal and rate-distortion verdicts go further — the incumbent *is* the mandatory verbatim life-raft, and Baumy's golden rule ("deterministic code disposes") is precisely the deterministic verbatim/audit gate that the generative frame theoretically *requires*. **The security spine is not challenged; it is re-justified from memory theory.**

In one line: a **CLS hybrid** — reconstructive/relationship layer as the primary conversational recall surface, the incumbent knowledge-graph as its firewalled records/consolidation spine.

## V. How this refines and challenges our baseline

Mapped to the incumbent's actual components (`captureMemory`, `extractFacts`/`reconcileFact`/`resolveEntity`, `retrieve.ts`, `reflect.ts`, `forget.ts`, crypto, reminders):

- **Challenge 1 — Mis-framing, not wrongness (the headline).** The graph is right but *inverted*: it should be understood as the slow consolidated layer + fidelity/audit ledger, with **relationship-indexed reconstruction** as the organizing frame. We are not wrong; we are upside-down.

- **Challenge 2 — Missing fast episodic layer.** Atomic unit is the triple (+ evidence item); "who was there / how it felt / that night we argued" is discarded into edge metadata. 5/5 + instance-primary + what-where-when say **episodes with affect + temporal context should be first-class.**

- **Challenge 3 — Missing reconstruction read step.** `retrieve.ts` (even the deep-tier rerank) *ranks and returns*; it never *regenerates gist-first in-voice*. This is the 5/5 top omission and the direct cause of any lookup feel.

- **Challenge 4 — The clean, conversation-specific omission.** No per-dyad common ground, no who-knows-what directory, no destination-memory anti-repetition ledger, no epistemic-rights tracking. Most apt missing piece for a *group* bot; also the safest to add (pointers/provenance, not reconstruction).

- **Challenge 5 — `reflect` is under-powered and mis-typed.** It flattens to a frozen `system`-trust profile string via supersession. The frames say: (a) it is the **learning engine** (generative replay — predictive-generative), central not housekeeping; (b) keep **vivid anchor episodes**, not only gist; (c) consider an **always-current read-time profile** (instance-primary) rather than a written fact that goes stale and destructively supersedes.

- **Challenge 6 — Ranking + forgetting.** Add **need-probability ranking + surprise-gating** (Anderson & Schooler 1991; rate-distortion) and **deliberate decay to gist** — with a privacy dividend (surveillance-avoidance).

- **Challenge 7 — Updating (a genuine strength, to be extended, not replaced).** Trust-gated bitemporal supersession is *convergence-with-incumbent* (3/5) and our poisoning defense is more concrete than the theorists' "transactive stance." But: (a) expose the **arc** conversationally instead of silent overwrite (narrative-self); (b) treat trust as **precision weight** — predict *when* a correction should stick (predictive-generative); (c) keep superseded values **recoverable under original-context cues** (instance-primary) rather than gone.

- **Challenge 8 — Output calibration.** We store trust/provenance but never **hedge by trace strength** or voice metamemory (4/5; workspace-attention). Add feeling-of-knowing / "you haven't told me that."

- **Where the incumbent WINS and must be preserved (the firewall — re-justified, not weakened).** Verbatim/auditable/one-shot/deletable facts (secrets, door codes, rent dates, promises), provenance, exactly-once reminders, trust-gating against attacker-controlled group text, the injection wall. Reconstruction is structurally unsafe here; *every* winning theorem walls itself out of this core. Chen et al. (ACNS 2023) confirms the write channel is a real attack surface.

**Net.** *Keep the graph; demote it from "the memory model" to "the fidelity + consolidation layer"; add on top — (a) a fast episodic layer, (b) a reconstruction read step, (c) a common-ground/transactive ledger, (d) need-probability ranking + adaptive forgetting, (e) a metamemory/hedging surface, (f) `reflect` reframed as the learning engine — and firewall all of it OUT of the secrets/reminders/authz core.*

**Final honesty (the caveat that governs all of the above).** This entire convergence was reasoned from the *cognitive-plausibility* direction and is blind to the engineering/IR/safety axis on which the incumbent was actually chosen. So "the incumbent under-weights process, relationship-indexing, and reconstruction" is a **robust** finding; "replace the graph" is **not**. The defensible conclusion is **re-frame and augment into a firewalled hybrid** — which is also exactly what the strongest theorems, scored adversarially, independently concluded.
