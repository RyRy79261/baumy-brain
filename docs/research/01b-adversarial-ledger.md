# Pass-1 claim ledger (flattened)

## T1-plural — verdict: mixed
- [weakened-but-stands / high] A) The working/long-term and declarative/procedural/episodic/semantic partition is SHARED across cognitive science (Tulving, Baddeley, Squire, Atkinson-Shiffrin) and AI cognitive architectures (CoALA <- Soar, ACT-R).
    - crux: How much genuinely independent warrant survives once the borrowed labels are stripped — i.e., would engineering pressure (bounded context vs external store; retrievable knowledge vs compiled policy/weights) have produced the fast/slow and declarative/procedural cuts even with zero contact with Tulving/Squire? That residue, not the label-sharing, is the only thing that could rescue any 'convergence' reading.
- [weakened-but-stands / high] B) Labeling an agent store 'episodic' is a productive functional ANALOGY, not evidence agents 'have' episodic memory in Tulving's sense.
    - crux: Under the operational what-where-when criterion (not autonoetic), do current agent memory stores actually satisfy episodic-LIKE memory — including the integrated, flexible, one-trial-bound representation animal work requires — or only a trivial 'queryable timestamped log' version that even the behavioral criterion would reject? The fuzziness the red team invokes may itself dissolve at the stricter operational reading.
- [survives / high] C) Whether these are separate systems vs a continuum (SPI / embedded-processes), and whether a distinct short-term store exists vs 'activated LTM', is itself contested in psychology.
    - crux: If working memory is activated LTM and recollection is an operation-at-retrieval rather than content in a dedicated subsystem, does a store-based ontology (n distinct memory stores) misdescribe the human architecture badly enough that ANY agent taxonomy built on discrete stores is analogizing to a structure a substantial camp says does not exist as discrete stores — regardless of the labels used?
## T2-encoding — verdict: mixed
- [weakened-but-stands / high] A) Mature capacity-bounded systems store transformed/separated derivatives as their operational memory, never the raw input stream (hippocampal pattern separation; facts/summaries in AI).
    - crux: Once 'transformed' is separated from 'verbatim-replay,' where exactly is the falsifiable line — does detail-rich persistence (Brady, MTT) count as 'raw' or merely as another transformed trace, and does the claim retain any bite beyond the analytic 'queryable ⇒ indexed'?
- [genuinely-contested / medium] B) Write-side rule 'extract freely, canonicalize precisely': over-merge is fatal/irreversible, under-merge is recoverable (precision-write, fuzzy-read), mirroring separate-precisely/complete-fuzzily in the brain.
    - crux: Is there a principled taxonomy of when precision-first actually dominates (loss function, provenance availability, notice-ability of the error) rather than a blanket law — and does provenance-preserving storage dissolve the asymmetry entirely or does silent under-merge remain the worse failure precisely because it emits no signal?
- [weakened-but-stands / high] C) What is encoded fixes which cues can retrieve it (encoding specificity / transfer-appropriate processing); levels-of-processing 'depth' is circular.
    - crux: Can 'processing match/appropriateness' be operationalized independently of the retrieval outcome to escape the circularity charge — and is Nairne's diagnosticity a replacement for encoding-specificity or a refinement that still leaves the 'distillation bounds reachable cues' design intuition intact?
- [genuinely-contested / medium] D) AI extraction is a discrete, errorful pipeline stage (hallucinated/omitted triples that compound downstream) with no biological analogue and usually no write-time trust model.
    - crux: Is 'encoding' one concept across substrates or two — i.e. can the cluster be rescued by declaring the convergence FUNCTIONAL (capacity-driven distillation) with divergent MECHANISMS, and if so must the 'convergence' language be downgraded uniformly for A and C, not just conceded in D?
## T3-weighting — verdict: mixed
- [weakened-but-stands / high] A) Graded, use-dependent, multi-signal weighting is the shared backbone of adaptive recall; Generative Agents' 3-factor score (recency × relevance × importance) is functionally ≈ ACT-R activation (base-level + spreading activation).
    - crux: Does any memory system's 'importance' term actually behave like ACT-R's use-history base-level (frequency of access), or is importance categorically a content-salience prior distinct in kind — such that the A-convergence holds ONLY at recency+relevance and fails at the third factor?
- [weakened-but-stands / high] B) ACT-R fan-normalization (down-weighting associative strength by log node degree) is a principled defense against hub domination in retrieval AND predicts the empirically robust human fan effect — the same normalization for the same information-theoretic reason.
    - crux: Is there a genuinely shared normative quantity behind inverse-degree down-weighting across cognition and retrieval, or is 'promiscuous nodes carry less weight' merely a convergent behavior reachable from a Bayesian, a geometric, and a linear-algebra derivation independently — i.e., is the parallel structural coincidence rather than shared cause?
- [weakened-but-stands / high] C) Human retrievability tracks environmental need-probability (Anderson & Schooler 1991); the unqualified 'near-optimal' framing is contested and should be read as bounded/resource-rational (Lieder & Griffiths 2020).
    - crux: Does the need-probability correspondence survive at the level of individual (unaveraged) memory and environmental recurrence curves, and would it hold for non-Zipfian / non-human-text environments — i.e., can it be separated from the averaging artifact and the same-statistical-family selection?
- [survives / high] D) Where 'importance' comes from is unresolved and no dominant standard has emerged (LLM-rated poignancy vs. access-count vs. graph centrality vs. calibrated confidence); there is no shared theory of what an edge weight MEANS, unlike recency and relevance which have normative anchors.
    - crux: Do the four importance proxies empirically correlate on shared items — i.e., are they measuring one latent utility/need-probability quantity (under-unification) or genuinely distinct constructs (incommensurability)? This is testable and would settle whether D is a deep semantic void or merely an un-unified engineering knob.
## T4-traversal — verdict: mixed
- [weakened-but-stands / medium] A) The deepest cross-field convergence is spreading activation (Collins & Loftus) = Personalized PageRank (HippoRAG) = Hopfield completion, where attention IS one-step Hopfield retrieval, exactly.
    - crux: Does hetero-associative (W_K != W_V), single-step attention still count as content-addressable completion, or is it a genuinely distinct primitive that the 'completion not lookup' framing cannot claim without dilution?
- [survives / high] B) Retrieval is cue-dependent, not strength-absolute: accessibility != availability (Tulving & Pearlstone 1966) and encoding specificity (Tulving & Thomson 1973), and this predicts the modern dense-retrieval failure mode measured by BEIR.
    - crux: Is there any mechanism-level (not merely analogical) link between encoding-context reinstatement in episodic memory and encoder-granularity loss in embedding retrieval, or is the bridge purely a mnemonic?
- [weakened-but-stands / medium] C) Practical convergence is hybrid fusion (semantic + lexical + graph via RRF) with complementarity by query type: graph wins multi-hop/global, flat wins single-fact.
    - crux: How much of the 'graph wins global/multi-hop' advantage survives when scored with hard, leakage-controlled metrics instead of LLM-as-judge, and is any surviving edge attributable to topology rather than to added hierarchy?
- [survives / high] D) 'Flat RAG structurally cannot do global' is overstated (RAPTOR is a topology-free counterexample); the active ingredient may be hierarchy, not graph topology - and this is unresolved.
    - crux: Is there (or can there be) a controlled ablation isolating graph topology while holding multi-level hierarchy constant - and does coarse-to-fine hierarchical routing belong under 'associative completion' or under 'addressing,' which is the crux of whether the unifying thesis is falsifiable?
## T5-consolidation — verdict: mixed
- [weakened-but-stands / high] Two-timescale design (fast sparse hippocampal encoder + slow generalizing neocortex) is the STRUCTURAL ANSWER to catastrophic interference, close to a theorem, and independently endorsed by ML continual-learning theory (EWC).
    - crux: Is scale/orthogonalization a genuinely distinct third route to interference-resistance, or does a large single network implement the same pattern-separation-vs-integration tradeoff CLS describes, just within one substrate? And is EWC's underperformance (near-floor in class-incremental benchmarks) evidence that the SYNAPTIC branch of the analogy specifically fails?
- [weakened-but-stands / medium] Offline replay/reflection TRANSFORMS episodes into gist (lossy, structure-extracting, distinct from storage), and AI analogues (hierarchical summarization/RAPTOR, sleep-time compute, chunking) implement the same offline pass.
    - crux: Is there ANY AI memory system whose abstraction is built by a lossy, read-time-emergent, detail-decaying transform (rather than write-time summaries or verbatim caching), such that the 'same offline structure-extracting operation' bridge is mechanistic rather than a shared abstract topology?
- [weakened-but-stands / high] Abstraction need not be stored — prototypes emerge at retrieval from exemplars (MINERVA-2); and its powerful form factorizes reusable structure from bound content (TEM), which is formally ≈ attention (the deepest cross-field result).
    - crux: Does the TEM–transformer correspondence survive for architectures NOT retrofitted with recurrent position encodings, and can any TEM-side generalization claim be separated from the grid-cell-emergence result that No-Free-Lunch shows to be tuning-dependent?
- [genuinely-contested / medium] The cracks (veridical-vs-generative replay; Tse 2007 fast cortical schema learning; systems-consolidation vs Multiple Trace Theory) REFINE rather than refute, each resolving in the cluster's favor.
    - crux: Can 'schema-consistency' be operationalized independently of observed learning rate (as Tse's pre-trained paradigm did, but generalized)? Without that, the amended CLS is unfalsifiable in most applications — and if MTT's trace-multiplication view is right, is 'consolidation-as-transfer' the wrong directional frame rather than an over-strong version of the right one?
## T6-updating — verdict: mixed
- [weakened-but-stands / high] Append-only is fatal; the field converges on a reconciliation policy (ADD/UPDATE/DELETE/NOOP) with bitemporal soft-invalidation, and this convergence is echoed independently by belief revision (AGM) and temporal databases (SQL:2011).
    - crux: Does the placement of the supersession policy (reconcile-at-read vs reconcile-at-write) have any measurable behavioral consequence, or is it a purely representational choice with no empirical stakes?
- [genuinely-contested / high] The human analogue is reconsolidation (update-on-read) + the misinformation effect, and 'retrieval is a write' (testing effect, RIF); update-on-read is intrinsic in humans but optional in machines, with reconsolidation boundary conditions contested.
    - crux: If the misinformation effect reflects coexistence/source-monitoring rather than trace alteration, does the human literature on net argue for retaining superseded state rather than overwriting it — and does any human finding license a specific machine update policy at all?
- [survives / high] Mechanistic (parametric) editing does not propagate local edits to logical neighbors (ripple problem, RippleEdits); cumulative edits cause spectral/norm-growth collapse; AlphaEdit only delays this, and only under the linear associative-memory approximation.
    - crux: Do non-parametric memory stores genuinely escape the ripple problem, or merely relocate it to retrieval/reconciliation — and will the spectral-corruption mechanism survive peer review, or is it a preprint-stage hypothesis atop a solid phenomenon?
- [weakened-but-stands / high] There is no consensus on hard-delete vs soft-invalidate, and whether in-place editing is ever safe maintenance at scale is unresolved — a no-consensus mirrored across machine unlearning and temporal-DB/GDPR tensions.
    - crux: Is there any technically decidable question hiding inside 'delete vs invalidate,' or is the choice fully determined by exogenous objectives (audit, legal erasure, provenance) with no field-internal correct answer?
## T7-degradation — verdict: robust
- [survives / high] A) Forgetting is lawful/negatively-accelerated (Ebbinghaus); spacing + testing robustly improve retention — among the most replicated effects in psychology.
    - crux: At the individual (non-aggregated) level, is the forgetting function power/log or exponential-to-an-asymptote — and does a genuine plateau (permanent memories) undercut using 'negatively-accelerated decay' as the constraint that discriminates Claim-B mechanism models?
- [weakened-but-stands / high] B) The mechanism of forgetting is unresolved after a century — time-decay (ACT-R) vs interference/discriminability (SIMPLE) vs context-drift (TCM) vs active erasure (Rac1/neurogenesis), each making different predictions.
    - crux: Is the decay-vs-interference question empirically decidable from behavioral data at all, and does the Marr-levels critique fully dissolve the rivalry — or do at least two camps (same-level decay vs interference; TCM-reversible vs Rac1-erased) still make a genuinely divergent, testable prediction about recoverability?
- [weakened-but-stands / medium] C) Some forgetting is adaptive — intermediate decay maximizes inference (Schooler & Hertwig); transience aids generalization (Richards & Frankland); the target is 'degrade well,' not retain-all.
    - crux: Beyond 'some forgetting can be beneficial,' is there any direct measurement (not simulation or analogy) that biological transience implements a regularization-like capacity constraint, or that any real agent's forgetting rate sits near the Schooler–Hertwig optimum?
- [survives / high] D) AI is thinnest here: it handles contradiction (invalidation) but has almost no principled decay of stale-yet-uncontradicted facts; whether time-based decay improves task performance is empirically unsettled (mixed ablations).
    - crux: Which human forgetting rationale actually transfers to machines — is it only the environmental-need-odds/rational-analysis strand, with the biological capacity and interference rationales being non-binding for lossless storage, and does that reframe what 'graceful forgetting for AI' should even optimize?
## T8-analogy-breaks — verdict: mixed
- [weakened-but-stands / high] A: LLM memory is bifurcated into two substrates (parametric weights vs non-parametric KV-cache/context) that degrade by categorically different, write-driven (not time-driven) laws — interference/spectral-collapse vs eviction/positional-starvation — and humans have no clean parametric/non-parametric split.
    - crux: After subtracting the calibratable positional bias and the sink-token fix, is there any irreducible occupancy/eviction term left in the non-parametric substrate — i.e. does a genuine substrate-specific 'failure law' survive at all, or does only the frozen-weights/volatile-buffer architectural gate remain as the disanalogy?
- [genuinely-contested / medium] B: 'Where is a fact stored?' may be ill-posed in LLMs — edit success is uncorrelated with causal-tracing localization (Hase et al. 2023), and the knowledge-neuron thesis (Dai et al. 2022) is empirically undercut.
    - crux: Is the read/edit-locus dissociation genuinely shared with biological engrams (making B a convergence), or is the fact that it is quantified, reproducible, and operationally exploitable in LLMs an LLM-specific EPISTEMIC affordance that constitutes a real methodological disanalogy even if the underlying distributed structure is shared?
- [weakened-but-stands / high] C: A writable memory (RAG store / agent long-term memory) is an attack surface with no biological analogue — untrusted inference-time input causes durable, privileged, retrieved-as-trusted state, and there is no standard write-trust defense.
    - crux: Given that Shaw-style implantation shares the threat-model shape, is the LLM disanalogy best stated as a quantitative gap (reliability/directness/ungatedness) rather than a categorical one — and does any biological process exist that installs a verbatim, immediately-retrievable, full-trust record in a single exposure (which would collapse the break further)?
- [weakened-but-stands / high] D: Elegant 'LLM = classical memory model' equivalences (attention=modern Hopfield; SAE latents = the model's concepts) may be mathematical re-description, not demonstrated mechanism; and long-context-as-solvent-of-retrieval-structure is genuinely open.
    - crux: Does the metric-failure Heap et al. actually demonstrate generalize to circuit-level / causal interpretability methods that do not rely on auto-interp scoring, and does the attention↔Hopfield function-class constraint get EXERCISED as the operative retrieval dynamic in trained models or merely bounds a latent capacity?
